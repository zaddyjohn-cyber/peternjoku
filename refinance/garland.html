Refinance Options

Four ways Garland homeowners refinance

Rate/Term Refinance

Lower your interest rate, shorten your loan term, or both. The goal is to reduce your monthly payment or total interest paid over time. Best for homeowners who bought or last refinanced when rates were higher. I calculate your break-even point — the month when savings exceed closing costs — before you decide.

Cash-Out Refinance (Texas 50(a)(6))

Access your home equity in cash — for renovations, debt consolidation, investment, or any purpose. Texas law caps cash-out refis at 80% LTV (stricter than most states). On a Garland home appraised at $320,000, the maximum you could access is $256,000 minus your remaining loan balance.

FHA Streamline Refinance

If you already have an FHA loan, the streamline program lets you refinance with no appraisal, minimal documentation, and a simplified underwriting process — as long as the new loan demonstrably benefits you (lower rate or reduced MIP). Garland has a high concentration of FHA borrowers who are good candidates.

VA IRRRL (Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan)

Veterans and active-duty homeowners with an existing VA loan can streamline into a lower rate with no appraisal and no income verification in most cases. The new rate must be lower than the current rate (except when refinancing an ARM to a fixed). I handle VA IRRRLs for Garland veterans regularly.

The Break-Even Calculation

Does refinancing make sense for your Garland home?

The break-even point is how long it takes for your monthly savings to exceed what you paid in closing costs. If you plan to stay in your Garland home past that point, refinancing is likely a smart move. If you're planning to sell in 2–3 years, it may not be.

What is the Texas cash-out refinance rule and how does it affect Garland homeowners?

Texas limits cash-out refinances to 80% of your home's appraised value under Article XVI Section 50(a)(6) of the Texas Constitution — stricter than other states.
