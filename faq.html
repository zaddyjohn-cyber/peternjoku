Frequently Asked Questions

Your Mortgage Questions, Answered

Common questions Texas homebuyers ask us — about down payment, credit, timing, documents, and the loan process.

How much down payment do I need?
It depends on the loan program. FHA can start as low as 3.5%, VA and USDA may offer 0% for qualified borrowers, and Conventional can start around 3%–5% for many primary residences. We will help you pick the program that fits.
What credit score is required?
Different loan programs have different credit guidelines. FHA tends to be more flexible than Conventional. There’s no single “magic number” — your full profile matters. We will review and explain what may qualify.
How long does mortgage approval take?
A pre-approval letter can often be issued within a few business days once documents are received. From contract to closing typically takes a few weeks, depending on the property and program.
Can I buy a home with student loans?
Yes — many buyers do. Student loan payments are factored into your debt-to-income ratio. We will walk through how your specific loans are counted.
What documents are needed?
Typically: ID, Social Security info, recent pay stubs, W-2s or tax returns (2 years), bank/asset statements, and a summary of debts. We will send a checklist tailored to your loan program.
What is the difference between pre-qualification and pre-approval?
Pre-qualification is a quick estimate based on what you tell us. Pre-approval is a more thorough review based on verified documents, and it’s what most sellers expect to see with a strong offer.
Can I refinance if rates change?
Possibly. Refinancing makes sense when the long-term savings outweigh the costs. We will run the math with your real numbers.
Do I need perfect credit to buy a home?
No. Many qualified buyers have less-than-perfect credit. FHA in particular is more flexible. We can help you understand what may qualify and whether short-term improvements would unlock better terms.
What loan program is best for me?
It depends on your goals, credit, savings, target home, and eligibility. A short consultation will narrow it down quickly — there’s no fee to ask.
Can I get pre-approved before I have a Realtor?
Absolutely. Many buyers prefer to know their budget first. We can also refer you to trusted local Realtors if you’d like.
Does Bond Peter work outside North Texas?
We are licensed to help borrowers throughout Texas. North Texas (DFW & surrounding areas) is his primary market.
