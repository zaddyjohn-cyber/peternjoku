Mortgage Broker vs. Mortgage Loan Officer — What's the Difference?

People use these terms interchangeably, and the distinction matters less than most think. A mortgage broker technically shops your loan across multiple wholesale lenders. A mortgage loan officer (MLO) is licensed to originate loans and typically works through one lender or a set of investor relationships.

What actually matters: does the person you're working with have access to the loan programs you need (FHA, VA, USDA, conventional), the experience to structure your file correctly, and the communication style that keeps you informed throughout the process?

What a Local Garland Mortgage Broker Does Better Than an Online Lender

National online lenders have their advantages — speed, technology, sometimes lower advertised rates. But Garland homebuyers consistently run into situations where local knowledge matters more than any algorithm:

  • Seller concession strategy: In Garland's current market, knowing whether to ask for seller concessions on a specific street or zip code requires local deal experience — not data pulled from a national dashboard.
  • Local appraiser relationships: Appraisal issues are the #1 deal killer in DFW. A local lender who has closed deals in Garland ZIP codes 75040, 75041, 75042, and 75043 knows what appraisers tend to flag and how to address it before it becomes a problem.
  • Title company coordination: Garland has its own rhythm for closings. Working with a loan officer who has an existing relationship with local title companies means fewer delays and clearer communication.
  • Down payment assistance access: TSAHC, TDHCA, and Dallas DHAP programs are geographically specific. A local lender knows which programs apply to your purchase address and whether you can stack them.

5 Questions to Ask Any Mortgage Broker Near You in Garland

  1. Are you NMLS licensed in Texas? Every mortgage loan originator must be individually licensed. You can verify any MLO at nmlsconsumeraccess.org. My NMLS number is #2670329.
  2. What loan programs do you offer? You want access to FHA, VA, USDA, conventional, and ideally down payment assistance programs. If a lender only offers one or two products, they may push you toward what they have rather than what fits.
  3. How fast can you close? In a competitive Garland market, a 30–35 day close matters. Ask how many loans they closed last month and what their average time-to-close was.
  4. Will I work with you directly, or get handed off to a processor? Some lenders use your initial call to lock you in, then pass you to a junior processor. Ask who you'll be talking to at every stage.
  5. Can you tell me about TSAHC or Dallas DHAP for my situation? A knowledgeable local MLO should immediately know what these programs are and whether you likely qualify — without having to Google it.

Down Payment Assistance Programs in Garland, TX

One of the biggest advantages of working with a local Garland loan officer is access to stacked assistance programs. Garland buyers may qualify for:

  • TSAHC Home Sweet Texas: 3–5% grant (not a loan — never repaid). Available to any qualified buyer, not just first-timers. Works with FHA, VA, USDA, and conventional loans.
  • TDHCA My First Texas Home: Up to 5% DPA plus a below-market 30-year rate. First-time buyers and veterans. Income limits apply.
  • Dallas DHAP: Up to $60,000 for qualified buyers purchasing within Dallas city limits. Income and purchase price limits apply. This program requires a TDHCA-certified lender.
