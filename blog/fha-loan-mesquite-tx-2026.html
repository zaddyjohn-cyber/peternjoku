FHA Loan Mesquite TX 2026 — Low Down Payment for East DFW Buyers

FHA LoansBy Bond Peter NjokuJuly 15, 20267 min read

I'm Bond Peter Njoku (NMLS #2670329), and Mesquite is one of the most FHA-friendly markets in the DFW Metroplex. Home prices here — many in the $200,000–$320,000 range — sit well inside Dallas County's FHA loan limits, the minimum credit score requirement is one of the lowest in any loan program, and when you layer in Texas down payment assistance, many Mesquite buyers get to the closing table with very little out of pocket. Here's everything you need to know about using an FHA loan to buy in Mesquite in 2026.

FHA Loan Limits in Mesquite, TX (Dallas County) 2026

Mesquite is in Dallas County. The 2026 FHA loan limit for a single-family home in Dallas County is $524,225. For most Mesquite buyers purchasing in the $200K–$350K range, you'll never get close to that ceiling. FHA limits are set by the Federal Housing Administration annually based on local median home prices — Dallas County's limit is higher than the national floor because of the metro's price appreciation.

FHA Requirements for Mesquite Buyers

  • Minimum credit score: 580 for 3.5% down · 500–579 for 10% down
  • Down payment: 3.5% of the purchase price (e.g., $9,275 on a $265,000 home)
  • Debt-to-income ratio: Up to 43–50% with compensating factors
  • Employment: 2 years of steady employment history (same field, not necessarily same employer)
  • Mortgage insurance: Upfront MIP of 1.75% + annual MIP of ~0.55–0.85% depending on term and LTV
  • Property condition: Home must meet FHA minimum property standards — no major structural issues or safety hazards

Stacking FHA With Down Payment Assistance in Mesquite

The most powerful combination I offer Mesquite buyers is FHA + TSAHC or TDHCA. These are state-run programs that provide money toward your down payment — on top of the FHA loan, not instead of it.

Real example on a $265,000 Mesquite home: FHA requires $9,275 (3.5%) down. TSAHC's 5% grant on a $256,725 loan = ~$12,836 — covers the down payment entirely, with the remainder offsetting closing costs. Out of pocket: potentially $0 on the down payment.

Most DPA programs require a 620+ credit score even though FHA allows 580. If you're at 580–619, you can still use FHA alone — the down payment is just coming from your own funds or a gift.

FHA vs Conventional in Mesquite — Which Is Better?

The right answer depends on your credit score and how long you plan to stay in the home.

If your score is 680 or above and you have 5% down, conventional often beats FHA on total cost because PMI drops once you hit 20% equity — FHA MIP stays for the life of the loan if you put less than 10% down. I run both scenarios for every Mesquite buyer I work with so you can see the real number difference.

What I Look for Before Pre-Approving a Mesquite FHA Buyer

Before I issue a pre-approval letter, I look at: your credit report (not just the score — the history matters), two years of tax returns and W-2s or 1099s if self-employed, your last two months of bank statements for the down payment source, and your current debt load. Most Mesquite buyers I work with get pre-approved within 24–48 hours of submitting their documents. The most common delay is incomplete income documentation — especially for buyers with gig work or self-employment income.
