Can I Buy a House With Bad Credit in Texas?

Credit By Peter June 28, 2026 6 min read

The short answer is: it depends on how low your credit score is and which loan program you're looking at. "Bad credit" means different things to different lenders, and there are real options available in Texas for buyers who don't have perfect credit histories. Here's an honest breakdown of what's possible, what's not, and what you can do right now to improve your odds.

What "Bad Credit" Actually Means in a Mortgage Context

Lenders don't use a single definition of "bad credit" — they use score thresholds tied to specific loan programs. Here's how the ranges translate to real options:

  • Below 500: Very few legitimate mortgage options exist at this level. Most programs require a minimum of 500, and lenders that will go this low are rare. Focus on credit improvement before applying.
  • 500–579: Limited options. FHA loans are technically available in this range, but require a 10% down payment — which is a significant barrier for most buyers. Expect higher rates and more scrutiny.
  • 580–619: More options open up. FHA loans are available with 3.5% down at this level. Some VA lenders will work with scores in this range. Conventional loans are generally not available below 620.
  • 620–659: Conventional loans become available, though rates will be higher than for buyers in the 700+ range. This is a workable range for motivated buyers — particularly those using FHA or VA programs.
  • 660+: Broader conventional access with improving terms. By 700+, you're accessing the best rate tiers most lenders offer.

FHA Loans: The Most Accessible Option for Lower Credit Scores

FHA loans are the most commonly used path for Texas buyers with lower credit scores. They're backed by the Federal Housing Administration and allow lenders to be more flexible with credit requirements than conventional programs.

  • 580+ credit score: Eligible for 3.5% down payment FHA loan
  • 500–579 credit score: Eligible for FHA with 10% down payment
  • More flexible on debt-to-income ratios than conventional loans
  • Gift funds are allowed for the down payment

The trade-off with FHA: you'll pay mortgage insurance premium (MIP) for the life of the loan if you put less than 10% down. Once you've built equity, refinancing into a conventional loan can remove that cost. Many Texas buyers use FHA to get into a home now and refinance later when their credit and equity improve.

VA Loans: The Best Option If You've Served

If you're an eligible veteran, active-duty service member, or surviving spouse, VA loans offer the most favorable terms of any mortgage program — including for buyers with lower credit scores.

  • The VA itself does not set a minimum credit score requirement
  • Most VA lenders in Texas work with scores of 580–620 as a practical minimum
  • No down payment required
  • No private mortgage insurance
  • Generally lower interest rates than FHA or conventional

If you've served and have less-than-perfect credit, the VA loan is often the most powerful tool available to you. We work with VA borrowers regularly and understand the nuances of how lenders evaluate these files.

USDA Loans: Rural and Suburban Areas of Texas

USDA loans are available in many suburban and rural areas of Texas — including some communities in the outer DFW ring — and offer 100% financing with no down payment. However, credit requirements are somewhat stricter: most USDA lenders require a minimum score of 640, and income limits apply.

If you're considering a home in areas like Forney, Royse City, or other outer suburbs, it's worth checking whether the property and your income qualify for USDA. We can run eligibility in minutes.

Steps to Take Right Now If Your Credit Is Too Low

If your score isn't at the minimum threshold yet, here's the highest-impact sequence of actions:

  1. Get your free credit reports. Pull all three bureaus at AnnualCreditReport.com. Review every line for errors — wrong balances, accounts that aren't yours, late payments that were actually on time. Errors are more common than most people realize.
  2. Dispute any errors immediately. File disputes directly with each bureau online. Legitimate errors must be investigated within 30 days. Removing an incorrect late payment or collection can raise a score by 20–50 points in some cases.
  3. Pay down revolving balances. Credit utilization — how much of your available revolving credit you're using — is one of the biggest factors in your score. Getting utilization below 30% (ideally below 10%) can produce fast, meaningful score gains.
  4. Set up autopay for every account. A single 30-day late payment can drop a score by 60–100 points. Eliminate that risk entirely by automating minimum payments before you're in active home-search mode.
  5. Don't open new credit accounts. New inquiries and new accounts lower your average account age and introduce risk flags. Hold off on any new credit until after you've closed on your home.
  6. Don't close old accounts. Even unused accounts contribute to your available credit limit and account history length — both of which support your score.

Our credit score improvement guide goes deeper on each of these steps with specific tactics for Texas buyers.

How Long Until You Can Qualify?

The timeline is shorter than most people expect when the right steps are taken consistently. Here's a realistic picture:

  • Error disputes resolved: 30–60 days after filing
  • Utilization paydown reflected: 30–45 days after the balance drops (next reporting cycle)
  • Meaningful overall improvement: Typically 3–6 months of positive, consistent behavior
  • Significant improvement from a very low score: 6–12 months for more severe situations (recent late payments, collections, etc.)

We've helped buyers go from a 560 to a qualifying 580+ in under 90 days with the right targeted strategy. The key is knowing exactly which accounts to address first — which is something we can help you identify during a no-pressure credit review.

Down Payment Assistance Programs With Flexible Credit Requirements

Texas has several down payment assistance programs through the Texas State Affordable Housing Corporation (TSAHC) and the Texas Department of Housing and Community Affairs (TDHCA). Some of these programs are designed specifically for buyers with lower credit scores and modest incomes, and they can provide grants or forgivable second loans to cover your down payment and closing costs.

Combining an FHA loan with a DPA grant is one of the most effective strategies we use for buyers who are credit-challenged and short on savings. Call us at 469-545-7180 and we'll tell you which programs you qualify for right now — and what it would take to qualify for more.

Frequently Asked Questions

What credit score do I need to buy a house in Texas?

The minimum credit score depends on the loan program. FHA loans accept scores as low as 580 with 3.5% down, or 500–579 with 10% down. Conventional loans typically require a minimum of 620. VA loans have no official minimum score set by the VA, but most lenders want at least 580–620. USDA loans generally require a 640 or higher. If your score is below these thresholds, a focused credit improvement plan can often get you to qualifying range within 3–6 months.

Can I get an FHA loan in Texas with a 580 credit score?

Yes. FHA loans are available in Texas with a minimum 580 credit score and a 3.5% down payment. If your score is between 500 and 579, you may still qualify but will need a 10% down payment. FHA loans are often the best entry point for buyers with lower scores because they have more flexible underwriting than conventional loans and are available throughout the DFW area.

How long does it take to improve credit enough to buy a house in Texas?

Most buyers can achieve meaningful credit score improvement within 3 to 6 months when they take the right steps consistently — paying down revolving balances, disputing errors, making all payments on time, and avoiding new credit applications. The exact timeline depends on what's dragging the score down. Errors can be resolved in weeks; building positive payment history takes longer. We help clients map out a specific plan based on what's actually on their report.
