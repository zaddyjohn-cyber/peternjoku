Conventional Loan Requirements in Richardson TX for 2026

Conventional loans follow Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac guidelines statewide. Here's what you need to qualify in Richardson in 2026:

  • Credit score: 620 minimum; 680+ recommended for the best rate tiers
  • Down payment: 3% minimum (HomeReady/Home Possible); 5–10% most common in Richardson
  • Debt-to-income ratio: Generally 43–45% max; up to 50% with compensating factors
  • Employment: 2 years of verifiable employment or self-employment history
  • Loan amount: Up to $806,500 (Dallas/Collin County conforming limit, 2026)
  • Property type: Single-family, condos, townhomes, 2–4 unit properties

Richardson has a mix of housing stock, from 1970s ranch homes to newer infill construction. Conventional loans give you more flexibility on condition issues that might trip up an FHA appraisal, which matters if you're eyeing one of Richardson's established neighborhoods that could use minor updates.

Conventional vs. FHA in Richardson TX — Which Is Right for You?

I model both loan types for every Richardson buyer. My general rule of thumb for 2026:

  • Go conventional if: Credit score 680+, putting down 5% or more, buying a well-maintained home
  • Consider FHA if: Credit score 580–639, minimal down payment, or the property has condition issues an FHA appraiser might flag differently
  • VA loan if eligible: Always my first recommendation for eligible veterans — $0 down, no PMI

For Richardson's price range, the PMI-cancellation advantage of conventional financing typically wins out for buyers who clear 680 credit and can put down at least 5%. I'll show you the 10-year cost comparison when we talk.
