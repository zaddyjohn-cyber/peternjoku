Does Royse City TX Qualify for a USDA Loan?

Yes. Royse City is located in Rockwall County and qualifies for the USDA Section 502 Guaranteed Loan Program in 2026. The USDA designates eligible areas based on population size and urban classification — Royse City's outer areas and most of its residential streets meet the criteria for rural designation despite the city's rapid growth.

That said, eligibility is always address-specific. As Royse City has expanded, some areas closer to established corridors may have been reclassified. I verify every Royse City property address on the official USDA map before we start — don't assume based on city alone. To check yourself, go to eligibility.sc.egov.usda.gov, click "Single Family Housing Guaranteed," and enter the full property address.

How USDA Financing Works — The Numbers

USDA loans come with two costs that replace a down payment and PMI:

  • Upfront guarantee fee: 1% of the loan amount, rolled into the loan (no cash needed at closing)
  • Annual fee: 0.35% of the loan balance per year, billed monthly — about $82/month on a $280,000 loan

That annual fee is significantly lower than FHA's mortgage insurance premium (~$158/month on the same loan amount). On a $280,000 loan, USDA saves roughly $76/month over FHA — that's $912/year, or over $27,000 across a 30-year loan.

What Properties Qualify in Royse City

The home must be your primary residence — USDA doesn't finance investment properties or vacation homes. The property must be in move-in condition (USDA has minimum property standards similar to FHA). Most standard single-family homes and newer townhomes in Royse City qualify without issue. Major distressed properties or fixer-uppers are harder to finance with USDA.

There is no maximum loan amount set by USDA itself — you're limited by what you can qualify for based on your income and debt-to-income ratio, not an arbitrary ceiling. This makes USDA especially useful in Royse City where prices have been rising.
