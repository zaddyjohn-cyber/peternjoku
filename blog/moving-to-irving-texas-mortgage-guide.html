Moving to Irving, TX — Mortgage Guide for 2026 Buyers

TX Real EstateBy Bond Peter NjokuJuly 14, 20268 min read

I'm Bond Peter Njoku, and if you're relocating to Irving, you've picked one of the more central spots in DFW — sandwiched between Dallas and Fort Worth, home to DFW Airport, Las Colinas, and a job market that pulls people in from across the metroplex and the country. I work with relocating buyers every month who are trying to figure out financing from a distance, sometimes before they've even seen a home in person. Here's what you actually need to know about buying in Irving in 2026.

What Homes Cost in Irving Right Now

Irving's housing stock runs the gamut — from condos and townhomes near Las Colinas to single-family homes in established neighborhoods further south and west. DFW-wide, median prices have been trending up modestly while new-home prices have actually softened, giving buyers more room to negotiate than they had a year or two ago. Days on market are also running longer across the metroplex, which works in your favor if you're relocating and need time to compare options rather than rushing an offer.

Loan Programs That Work Well for Irving Buyers

  • Conventional loans: The most common choice for Irving buyers with solid credit (680+) and steady income, especially for homes in the $350K-$550K range near Las Colinas.
  • FHA loans: A strong option if your credit is in the 580-660 range or you want a lower down payment (3.5%). Works well on the more affordable end of Irving's market.
  • VA loans: With DFW's military and veteran population, VA financing with zero down is common in Irving — no PMI, competitive rates.
  • Down payment assistance: Statewide programs through TSAHC and TDHCA are available to Irving buyers regardless of city — they're not restricted to specific ZIP codes.

Buying From Out of State or Out of Town

A large share of my relocating clients handle 80-90% of the mortgage process remotely — document upload, e-signatures, and video or phone calls instead of in-person meetings. If you're moving to Irving for a job at one of the corporate campuses near Las Colinas, the biggest thing I can do for you is get you pre-approved before you fly in, so you can make competitive offers the moment you find the right home instead of losing it while financing gets sorted out.

Neighborhood Considerations That Affect Your Loan

Irving has a mix of property types that can affect your financing path. Condos and some townhome developments may require additional condo project approval for conventional financing (HOA budget review, owner-occupancy ratios), while single-family homes in most Irving neighborhoods finance more straightforwardly. If you're eyeing a specific building or subdivision, I can check its approval status before you write an offer so there are no surprises mid-contract.
