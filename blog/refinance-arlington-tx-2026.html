Cash-Out Refinance Rules in Arlington TX — Texas 50(a)(6)

Texas has unique rules for cash-out refinances that don't apply in other states, and I make sure every Arlington homeowner I work with understands them before we proceed.

Under Texas law (Article XVI, Section 50(a)(6)), a cash-out refinance on your primary residence is capped at 80% of the home's current appraised value. That means if your Arlington home appraises at $300,000, the maximum loan amount is $240,000 — and you receive the difference between that and your payoff balance in cash. There's also a mandatory 12-day waiting period between application and closing on Texas cash-outs.

When Does Refinancing in Arlington TX Make Sense in 2026?

I hear this question from Arlington homeowners constantly — "Is now the right time?" My honest answer: it depends on your numbers, not a general market call. Here's the framework I use with every client:

  • Rate reduction: If you're at 7%+ and I can get you to 6% or below, the monthly savings on a $260,000 balance is roughly $150–$220/month. Break-even on $5,500–$7,000 in closing costs: 28–36 months.
  • PMI removal: If you put down less than 20% and your Arlington home has appreciated to 80% LTV, refinancing to a new conventional loan drops PMI — saving $100–$200/month forever.
  • FHA to conventional: If you have an FHA loan from a few years ago and your credit and equity are both strong, switching to conventional removes the lifetime MIP. That's often $150–$250/month in savings.
  • Term shortening: If you can afford slightly higher payments and want to pay off your Arlington home faster, a 30→15 year refi at today's rates can save $70,000–$100,000 in total interest.

How to Refinance in Arlington TX — The Process

Refinancing with me as your Tarrant County lender is straightforward:

  1. Free consultation: I review your current loan, rate, balance, and home value. I'll tell you immediately whether refinancing makes financial sense.
  2. Application: You complete a loan application — takes about 20 minutes. I'll pull your credit and request income documentation.
  3. Appraisal (if needed): Most refinances require a new appraisal to establish current value. FHA streamlines and VA IRRRLs often skip this step.
  4. Underwriting: I submit your file and manage the process. Most files are in underwriting within 5–7 business days.
  5. Closing: You sign at a title company near your Arlington home. Rate/term refinances typically close in 20–30 days; cash-outs add the required 12-day Texas waiting period.

I'm reachable by phone, text, and WhatsApp throughout the process — not a call center. You'll know your loan officer's name and have a direct line. That matters when you have a question on a Tuesday evening before closing. Also see my Arlington mortgage page for local market context.

Refinance Costs in Arlington TX — What to Expect

Closing costs on a refinance in Arlington typically run 2–3% of the loan amount, covering the appraisal, title work, lender fees, and prepaid items (taxes, insurance). On a $270,000 refinance, that's roughly $5,400–$8,100. In many cases I can structure a no-closing-cost refinance where the costs are rolled into the rate — you get a slightly higher rate but zero out-of-pocket costs at closing. Whether that makes sense depends on how long you plan to stay in your home.

I'll show you the side-by-side comparison of paying costs upfront vs. no-cost structure before you decide. Call or text me at 469-545-7180 — I'll have numbers for you within the hour.
