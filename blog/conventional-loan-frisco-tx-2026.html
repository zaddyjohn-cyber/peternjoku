Conventional Loan Requirements in Frisco TX for 2026

Conventional loans follow Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac guidelines statewide, but Frisco's price point changes how they play out in practice. Here's what you need to qualify in 2026:

  • Credit score: 620 minimum; 700+ recommended for Frisco's competitive price range
  • Down payment: 3% minimum (HomeReady/Home Possible); 10–20% most common in Frisco
  • Debt-to-income ratio: Generally 43–45% max; up to 50% with compensating factors
  • Employment: 2 years of verifiable employment or self-employment history
  • Loan amount: Up to $806,500 (Collin County conforming limit, 2026)
  • Property type: Single-family, new construction, townhomes, and select condos

Frisco has a heavy mix of new construction, and conventional financing pairs well with builder contracts because it doesn't require the same strict condition standards FHA appraisers apply. I work with several Frisco-area builders and know how to structure financing around their construction timelines.

2026 Conventional Loan Limits in Frisco TX (Collin County)

Frisco sits primarily in Collin County, where the 2026 conforming loan limit is $806,500 for a single-family home. That covers the vast majority of Frisco purchases, including most new construction in the $500,000–$700,000 range.

If you're looking at a higher-end Frisco new build above $806,500, that moves into jumbo territory with different underwriting requirements — I can walk you through those too. For more on Frisco-specific loan options, see my Frisco conventional loans page, the main conventional loans guide, or my Frisco mortgage page.

Conventional vs. FHA for Frisco Move-Up Buyers

I model both loan types for every Frisco client, but the answer is usually clear given the buyer profile here. My general rule for 2026:

  • Go conventional if: Credit score 700+, putting down 10% or more, buying new construction or a well-maintained resale
  • Consider FHA if: Credit score under 640 or you need the lowest possible down payment — less common in Frisco but it happens
  • VA loan if eligible: Frisco has a growing veteran population — always my first recommendation if you qualify

On a $520,000 Frisco home, the PMI-cancellation advantage of conventional over FHA can save a buyer hundreds of dollars a month once they hit 80% equity — FHA mortgage insurance typically runs for the life of the loan. I'll show you the full 10-year comparison when we talk.
