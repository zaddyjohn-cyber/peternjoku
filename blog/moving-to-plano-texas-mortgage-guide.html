Moving to Plano, TX — Mortgage Guide for Relocating Buyers

TX Real EstateBy Bond Peter NjokuJuly 11, 20269 min read

Plano keeps landing on national "best places to live" lists for the same reasons DFW buyers already know: top-rated school districts, a deep corporate job base anchored by companies headquartered in Legacy West and along the Dallas North Tollway, and a suburban feel that's still a quick drive from downtown Dallas. If you're relocating for a job transfer or just chasing better schools, here's what actually matters about buying a home here — and how the mortgage side works if you're moving from out of state.

What to Expect on Price

Plano commands a premium over much of the DFW metro, and that premium tracks closely with school zone. West Plano and neighborhoods feeding into the most sought-after elementary and high schools sit at the top of the price range; East Plano and older sections near Highway 75 offer more accessible entry points into the same city. Townhomes and newer builds near Legacy West trade differently than established single-family neighborhoods, so it pays to know which trade-off — commute, school zone, or lot size — matters most to your family before you start touring.

Choosing the Right Loan Type for Plano's Price Points

Because Plano homes tend to sit above the county's FHA loan limit floor in many neighborhoods, most buyers here end up comparing conventional financing against FHA rather than defaulting straight to FHA the way a first-time buyer in a lower-priced suburb might. If you're relocating with VA eligibility, a VA loan is frequently the strongest option in Plano because it removes the down payment requirement entirely on a higher-priced purchase. I compare all three side by side so you're not guessing which one actually saves you the most over the life of the loan.

Relocating From Out of State — What's Different

If you're moving to Plano for a new job, the mortgage process has a few extra steps worth planning for:

  1. Get pre-approved remotely before your move. I pre-approve relocating buyers over phone, text, and email so you can start house-hunting the moment you land — or even schedule tours before you arrive.
  2. Line up your employment documentation early. A new job offer letter, especially across state lines, requires specific documentation that a local lender who handles relocations regularly will know how to structure correctly.
  3. Budget for Texas property taxes. Collin County tax rates are often higher than what out-of-state buyers are used to, and they directly affect your monthly payment — not just your purchase price.
  4. Ask about timing your closing with your move date. Coordinating a rent-back or a slightly delayed close can save you a temporary housing scramble.

Why Local Guidance Matters for a Plano Move

Plano's market moves differently than Garland or Mesquite — different price bands, different competing buyer pool, different pace. I serve all of DFW, including Plano, Frisco, McKinney, and Allen, and have walked relocating families through exactly this kind of cross-market move.
