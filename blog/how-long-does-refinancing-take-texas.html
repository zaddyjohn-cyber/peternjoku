How Long Does It Take to Refinance a Mortgage in Texas?

RefinancingBy PeterJuly 9, 20268 min read

Once a Lewisville homeowner decides refinancing makes sense, the next question is almost always: how long until it's actually done? The honest answer is 30 to 45 days for most refinances — but that number hides a lot of variability depending on your loan type, how fast you turn in documents, and whether you're pulling cash out. Here's the week-by-week breakdown.

The Typical Refinance Timeline

StageTypical Timeframe
Application & document collection1-3 days
Appraisal ordered & completed1-2 weeks
Underwriting review1-2 weeks
Clear to close & final documents3-5 days
Closing & funding1 day, plus rescission period if applicable
Total (rate-and-term)3-6 weeks

Texas's Cash-Out Refinance Waiting Period

If you're doing a cash-out refinance, Texas homestead law adds rules that don't apply to a standard rate-and-term refinance:

  • A mandatory 12-day waiting period between application and closing
  • You generally must wait at least 12 months between cash-out refinances on the same home
  • Cash-out refinances are capped at 80% of your home's appraised value under Texas law
  • A 3-day right of rescission after closing before funds are disbursed, similar to a standard refinance

These protections exist specifically because Texas has some of the strongest homestead protection laws in the country — they add a bit of time but they exist to protect homeowners.

What Speeds Up Your Refinance

  1. Have documents ready before you apply — pay stubs, W-2s or tax returns, bank statements, mortgage statement, and homeowners insurance declarations page
  2. Respond to underwriting requests within 24 hours — this is the single biggest lever borrowers control
  3. Schedule your appraisal immediately — appraiser availability is often the longest single wait in the process
  4. Avoid new credit or big purchases mid-process — new debt or credit inquiries can trigger a re-verification that adds days

What Slows It Down

The most common delays: slow document turnaround from the borrower, appraisal value disputes, title issues on older Texas homes (especially in established Dallas and Fort Worth neighborhoods), and incomplete income documentation for self-employed borrowers. If you know any of these might apply to your situation, flag it with your loan officer on day one rather than letting it surface mid-process.

Rate-and-Term vs. Cash-Out: Different Clocks

A straightforward rate-and-term refinance — lowering your rate or changing your term without taking cash out — moves through underwriting the same as a purchase loan, without the Texas 12-day waiting period. If your goal is simply to see whether refinancing makes sense at all, my guide on when refinancing your Texas mortgage makes sense covers the break-even math before you even start the clock.

FHA and VA Streamline Refinances Move Faster

If you already have an FHA or VA loan and you're simply lowering your rate without taking cash out, you may qualify for a streamline refinance. These programs skip a new appraisal in many cases and require less income documentation, since you're already an established borrower on the loan. A streamline refinance can sometimes close in as little as 2 to 3 weeks — noticeably faster than a standard rate-and-term refinance. Not every homeowner qualifies, and lenders still apply their own credit and payment history requirements, but it's worth asking about if your current loan is FHA or VA.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to refinance a house in Texas?

Most Texas refinances close in 30 to 45 days from application, assuming documents are submitted promptly and the appraisal comes back without issues. A straightforward rate-and-term refinance with a responsive borrower can sometimes close in as little as 3 weeks. Cash-out refinances take a bit longer due to Texas's mandatory waiting period. Is there a waiting period to refinance in Texas?

Yes, for cash-out refinances specifically. Texas law requires a minimum 12-day waiting period after application before closing, and you generally must wait at least 12 months between cash-out refinances on the same home. Rate-and-term refinances without cash out do not have this specific waiting period, though standard processing time still applies. Visit bondmortgagesolutions.com for the full rundown.

What can delay a mortgage refinance in Texas?

The most common delays are slow document turnaround from the borrower, appraisal scheduling and value disputes, title issues on older homes, and incomplete income documentation for self-employed borrowers. Responding to lender requests within 24 hours is the single biggest factor borrowers control to keep a refinance on schedule.

Yes.
