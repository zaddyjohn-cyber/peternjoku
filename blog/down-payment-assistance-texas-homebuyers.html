Down Payment Assistance Options for Texas Homebuyers

First-Time Buyers By Bond Peter June 24, 2026 4 min read

The down payment is usually the single biggest hurdle for first-time Texas homebuyers. The good news is that several state and local programs exist to help bridge that gap. The complicated news is that program rules change, eligibility varies, and stacking them with the right loan program takes some planning. Here's a clear overview of what's out there and how to think about it.

What Down Payment Assistance Actually Looks Like

"Down payment assistance" (DPA) is an umbrella term that covers several different structures:

  • Grants — funds you don't have to repay if you meet the program's terms (usually staying in the home for a minimum period).
  • Forgivable second liens — a second loan recorded on the property that gets forgiven over time, often 5 to 10 years.
  • Deferred or repayable second liens — second loans that aren't due until you sell, refinance, or pay off the first mortgage.
  • Closing cost assistance — sometimes bundled into the same program to cover closing costs alongside the down payment.

Texas Statewide Programs

Several statewide programs serve Texas homebuyers. Eligibility and terms vary, and the active list changes from year to year. In broad strokes, programs typically require:

  • Household income within a published limit (often based on the county you're buying in).
  • A property purchase price within program limits.
  • You to occupy the home as your primary residence.
  • Completion of a homebuyer education course (usually online, a few hours).
  • A minimum credit score that varies by program.

Many statewide programs also have a "first-time buyer" component, but the definition of first-time is often "no ownership in the past three years" — not literally first time.

City and County Programs

Beyond statewide programs, some Texas cities and counties run their own assistance. These programs are often more generous but more restrictive on geography and income. If you're buying in a specific city, it's worth checking whether a local program exists for that zip.

Stacking DPA With Your Loan

DPA generally pairs well with most major loan programs:

  • FHA + DPA — a very common combination for first-time buyers. FHA loan details →
  • Conventional + DPA — works on many programs, especially for buyers with stronger credit. Conventional info →
  • VA + DPA — VA usually doesn't need DPA (no down payment required), but assistance can sometimes help with closing costs. VA loan info →
  • USDA + DPA — USDA already offers 100% financing, but DPA can help with closing costs. USDA info →

What to Watch For

DPA isn't free money in every case. A few things to verify before you commit:

  1. Read the recapture rules. Some programs claw back assistance if you sell within a certain period or generate gain above a threshold.
  2. Check the interest rate tradeoff. Some DPA programs come with a slightly higher first-mortgage rate. Run the math to make sure the overall deal is still favorable.
  3. Confirm timing. Some programs have funding caps and run out mid-year.
