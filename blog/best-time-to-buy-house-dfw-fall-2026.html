Best Time to Buy a House in DFW This Fall 2026 — Why Timing Matters

Home BuyingBy Bond Peter NjokuJuly 12, 20269 min read

I'm Bond Peter Njoku, and I get this question constantly from buyers who are house-hunting in the DFW heat right now: should I wait? My answer is usually the opposite of what people expect — if your finances are ready, fall is frequently one of the smartest windows to buy in DFW, and 2026 is shaping up to be a particularly good year for it. Here's why timing your purchase for September and October could put more money in your pocket than waiting for spring.

Why Fall Works in Your Favor

The spring and summer buying season brings out the largest pool of buyers all year, which means more competition for every listing and less room to negotiate. By fall, that competition thins out considerably — families with school-age kids have already moved to avoid a mid-year transfer, and the buyers who remain in the market tend to be serious, pre-approved, and ready to move. Sellers who are still listed in September and October are often more motivated too, whether because a home has sat on the market since spring or because they need to close before year-end for their own reasons.

2026 Adds an Extra Layer of Leverage

This fall isn't a typical fall for DFW. The metro has shifted toward a genuine buyer's market in 2026, with reports showing roughly twice as many sellers as buyers in parts of the region and months of supply climbing toward 7 — well above the 5 to 6 months that typically defines a balanced market. That means the seasonal advantage buyers usually get in the fall is stacking on top of an already buyer-favorable market, giving you two forces working in the same direction at once.

What You Can Ask For in a Fall Deal

  • Seller-paid closing costs. With more sellers competing for fewer buyers, concessions are back on the table in many DFW submarkets.
  • Rate buydown contributions. Some sellers will contribute toward a temporary or permanent rate buydown to make their listing more attractive.
  • Repair credits instead of price cuts. A softer market gives you room to negotiate inspection findings without losing the deal.
  • More flexible closing timelines. Motivated fall sellers are often more willing to work around your schedule.

Getting Ready Now

Timing only helps you if your financing is ready to move when you find the right home. I recommend getting pre-approved before you start touring so you know your exact budget and can write a competitive offer the moment you're ready — whether that's FHA, Conventional, VA, or a program layered with down payment assistance. Buyers who wait to get pre-approved until they've already found a home often lose valuable negotiating time in a market where sellers want certainty.
