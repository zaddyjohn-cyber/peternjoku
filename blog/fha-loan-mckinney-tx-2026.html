FHA Loan McKinney TX 2026 — Low Down Payment in Collin County

FHA LoansBy Bond Peter NjokuJuly 22, 20268 min read

I'm Bond Peter Njoku (NMLS #2670329), and McKinney is one of the most active markets I serve in Collin County. It's also one where first-time buyers frequently get priced out by focusing on the down payment number — they see $350,000–$450,000 homes and assume they need $17,500 or more to get started. FHA changes that math significantly. With 3.5% down and a stacked TSAHC grant, many McKinney buyers are getting into homes with far less out of pocket than they expected. Here's how it works.

Why FHA Works in McKinney

McKinney homes generally range from $310,000 entry-level townhomes up to $500,000+ in neighborhoods like Stonebridge Ranch, Eldorado, and Tucker Hill. FHA financing covers a wide range of that spectrum. The key advantages for McKinney buyers:

  • 3.5% minimum down payment — on a $350,000 home that's $12,250, versus $70,000 for 20% down
  • 580+ credit score — more accessible than conventional's typical 620 minimum
  • Gift funds allowed — family contributions can cover the entire down payment
  • DPA stacking — TSAHC and TDHCA programs stack with FHA to cover all or most of the down payment for eligible buyers

FHA + Down Payment Assistance in McKinney

This is where McKinney buyers get the biggest advantage. The TSAHC Home Sweet Texas program offers a grant of 3–5% of the loan amount with no repayment requirement and no first-time buyer restriction. Stack that with FHA's 3.5% minimum, and many buyers can cover the entire down payment with the TSAHC grant alone.

The TDHCA My First Texas Home program goes further — up to 5% of the loan amount, deferred for 30 years and forgiven at year 3 if you stay in the home. Collin County income limits for both programs are approximately $119,700 for 2026. See my full down payment assistance guide for eligibility details.

The result: a McKinney buyer earning under $119,700 who qualifies for FHA + TSAHC could potentially purchase a home with zero to minimal out-of-pocket down payment. I structure these combinations regularly for Collin County clients.

FHA vs. Conventional in McKinney — Which Is Right?

The answer depends on your credit score. Here's a clear breakdown:

Credit ScoreRecommended PathWhy
580–619FHAConventional requires 620+; FHA is the only option
620–659Usually FHAFHA MIP pricing better than conventional PMI at these scores
660–679Run bothDepends on loan size and down payment — I run both scenarios
680+Often ConventionalConventional PMI cancels at 20% equity; FHA MIP is often permanent

I always run both scenarios for McKinney buyers at the 640–700 range, because the better choice isn't always obvious and it depends on your specific loan amount, down payment, and how long you plan to stay in the home.

McKinney New Construction and FHA

McKinney has significant new construction activity from builders like Pulte, Meritage, David Weekley, and others. FHA works well for new construction in McKinney — the home is already in move-in condition so it passes FHA's property standards easily. Builders sometimes offer incentives (rate buydowns, closing cost credits) that I can evaluate alongside FHA to maximize your savings.

If you're considering new construction, I recommend getting pre-approved with me before visiting model homes. Builder financing can be competitive, but it's always worth having an independent pre-approval to compare — and to protect your interests during contract negotiation.
