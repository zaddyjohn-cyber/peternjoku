USDA Loan Forney TX 2026 — Zero Down in Kaufman County

USDA LoansBy Bond Peter NjokuJuly 15, 20267 min read

I'm Bond Peter Njoku (NMLS #2670329), and Forney is one of the fastest-growing cities in Kaufman County — and one of the most important USDA markets I serve east of Dallas. Portions of Forney remain USDA-eligible in 2026, which means qualified buyers can still get $0 down financing here. The critical word is "portions" — Forney's rapid growth has pushed some neighborhoods out of USDA eligibility, and the boundaries shift more frequently here than in smaller Kaufman County cities like Terrell or Kaufman. Here's what you need to know before you start shopping.

Is Forney USDA-Eligible in 2026?

Yes — partially. The outer and eastern fringes of Forney, particularly newer subdivisions built away from the city center, frequently qualify. Areas closer to the US-80 corridor and downtown Forney are more likely to be ineligible as the city has grown. This is the key difference between Forney and a city like Terrell: Terrell is almost entirely eligible, while Forney requires address-by-address verification before you make an offer.

Check any address at eligibility.sc.egov.usda.gov. Enter the exact property address — not just the city — and the map will confirm eligible or ineligible instantly. I also verify every address for my clients before we start the application.

USDA Income Limits for Forney (Kaufman County) 2026

Household SizeIncome Limit
1–4 people~$112,450
5–8 people~$148,450

USDA counts all adult household members' income — not just the borrowers on the loan. If your adult child lives in the home and earns income, it counts. But deductions are available: $480 per dependent under 18, documented childcare costs for children under 12, and medical expense deductions for elderly or disabled household members. These deductions regularly bring buyers under the limit who initially appear to exceed it.

Why Forney Buyers Choose USDA Over FHA

For an eligible Forney address, USDA wins on monthly payment almost every time. The annual guarantee fee (0.35%) is substantially lower than FHA's annual MIP (0.55–0.85%), and there's no down payment to come up with. On a $280,000 Forney home:

What If My Forney Address Doesn't Qualify?

It happens — Forney's growth has pushed several neighborhoods out of the eligible zone. If your specific address doesn't qualify, the best alternatives are:

  • FHA + TSAHC — FHA loan with TSAHC's 3–5% grant covers the down payment entirely for income-qualified buyers (620+ credit required for TSAHC)
  • Consider nearby Terrell — Terrell is fully USDA-eligible and offers lower home prices than Forney in many cases
  • Conventional with 3% down — if your credit is 680+ the conventional route avoids FHA's lifetime MIP

I always check the map first and present your best options based on the actual property — not assumptions about the city.
