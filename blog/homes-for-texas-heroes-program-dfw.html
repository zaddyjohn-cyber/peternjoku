Homes for Texas Heroes Program in DFW — Help for Teachers, Nurses & Veterans

Loan ProgramsBy Bond Peter NjokuJuly 11, 20269 min read

If you're a teacher in Garland ISD, a nurse working nights at a Dallas hospital, a Mesquite firefighter, or a veteran who served at Fort Hood, there's a Texas down payment program built specifically for you — and most DFW buyers have never heard of it. Homes for Texas Heroes pairs a below-market interest rate with real down payment assistance for the public servants who keep this region running. Here's exactly who qualifies, how much help is on the table, and how it works alongside FHA, VA, or conventional financing.

Who Actually Qualifies as a "Texas Hero"

Unlike some down payment programs that require you to be a first-time buyer, Homes for Texas Heroes is open to eligible professionals buying a primary residence anywhere in the state — including every city I serve across DFW. Eligible occupations include:

  • Public school teachers, teacher aides, school counselors, and school librarians
  • Police officers and corrections officers
  • Firefighters and EMS/paramedic personnel
  • Veterans and active-duty military (documented with a DD-214 or LES)
  • Nurses and allied health professionals employed by state or public healthcare facilities in many cases

Income limits apply and vary by county, so a teacher in Garland and a nurse in Rockwall may see slightly different limits. I check your household income against the current limit before you spend a dime on inspections or appraisals.

What You Actually Get

The program combines two benefits most DFW buyers assume are mutually exclusive: a below-market fixed rate on a 30-year mortgage, plus assistance toward your down payment and closing costs. That assistance can be structured as a grant or a second lien, depending on the specific bond program funding it at the time you apply.

How It Stacks With Other Loan Programs

Homes for Texas Heroes isn't a standalone loan — it's layered on top of your base mortgage. A veteran in Mesquite or Irving might combine it with a VA loan for zero down plus rate assistance. A teacher with a 620 credit score might pair it with an FHA loan to minimize upfront cash. Buyers with stronger credit sometimes use it alongside conventional financing to avoid extra mortgage insurance costs long-term. I run the comparison so you see the true monthly payment under each option before you commit.

Applying in Garland, Dallas, and the Surrounding Suburbs

The application process runs through a participating lender — I'm Bond Peter Njoku (NMLS #2670329), and I'm one — rather than directly through the state. The general steps look like this:

  1. Confirm your occupation and household income meet current eligibility limits
  2. Get pre-approved so you know your target price range before house-hunting
  3. Complete a HUD-approved homebuyer education course (required for most Texas DPA programs)
  4. Find your home and submit a full application with income and asset documentation
  5. Close with your below-market rate and assistance funds applied at the table

Buyers in Garland, Mesquite, and Irving tend to move fastest through this process because I'm based locally at 1919 S. Shiloh Rd in Garland and can meet in person when it speeds things up. If you're shopping outside those cities, review my full DFW down payment assistance guide and first-time homebuyer guide for programs that layer with Homes for Texas Heroes in your specific county.

Why This Program Gets Overlooked

Most DFW public servants assume down payment assistance is only for first-time buyers, or they've never had a loan officer bring it up because it takes extra paperwork on the lender's side. I process Homes for Texas Heroes regularly and know the income documentation reviewers want to see, which keeps files from bouncing back and delaying your closing. If you work in one of these fields and haven't asked about it, call or text me at 469-545-7180 before you assume you need 20% down like everyone else.
