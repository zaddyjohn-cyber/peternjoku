How to Prepare for Mortgage Pre-Approval in Texas

Mortgage Tips By Peter June 24, 2026 4 min read

In most Texas markets right now, a strong pre-approval letter is the difference between an offer that gets considered and one that gets passed over. Sellers and listing agents have learned to read the difference between a quick pre-qualification email and a real, lender-verified letter. Here's how we help our clients arrive at the closing table with the kind of pre-approval that wins.

Documents You'll Need

Before you make a list of dream neighborhoods, we'll need to verify income, assets, identity, and debt. The standard package looks like this:

  • Identity: driver's license and Social Security number.
  • Income: two most recent pay stubs, two years of W-2s, and (if self-employed) two years of tax returns.
  • Assets: two months of bank statements showing your down payment funds and reserves.
  • Debts: a quick summary of monthly obligations — auto loans, student loans, credit cards, child support if applicable.
  • Property goals: the city or zip you're targeting and a comfortable monthly payment range.

Credit Prep Before You Apply

Two or three weeks before we pull credit, take a quick look at your reports. You're not trying to game the score — you're checking for errors that could hurt you and avoiding moves that lenders will see as risk signals. Don't open new credit lines, don't co-sign anything, and don't make large purchases on existing cards. We'd rather see clean, boring activity for 60 days.

If your score is sitting just under a meaningful threshold, we'll tell you. Sometimes paying down a single revolving balance can move you into a better program tier without a wholesale credit rebuild.

What We Actually Review

Behind the scenes, we run your file through three lenses: capacity (can you afford the payment?), credit (is your history reliable?), and collateral (does the home support the loan?). Pre-approval covers the first two — the collateral piece happens after you have a contract.

We look at your debt-to-income ratio against the program's allowed limits, verify income through pay stubs and tax returns, and run your credit through automated underwriting. The result is a real, lender-verified letter — not a self-reported estimate.

Making Your Letter Strong

Your pre-approval letter should match the offer. If we issue you a letter for $400,000 and you offer $385,000, your Realtor can ask us to reissue the letter at the offer amount. It signals to the seller that you aren't stretching and that we've already verified you at that specific level.

We respond to Realtor requests for letter updates the same business day. That responsiveness matters when offers are due by 5 PM.

How Long It Takes

Once we have your documents, a clean pre-approval typically comes back within one to three business days. Files with self-employment or complex income take a bit longer because we're piecing together a stable picture, not just reading a W-2.

Frequently Asked Questions

What documents do I need for mortgage pre-approval in Texas?

For mortgage pre-approval in Texas you typically need: a government-issued photo ID and Social Security number; the two most recent pay stubs; two years of W-2s (or two years of tax returns if self-employed); two months of bank statements showing your down payment funds; and a general summary of your monthly debts. The lender will pull your credit report directly. Having these documents organized before you apply speeds up the process significantly.

How long does mortgage pre-approval take in Texas?

A clean pre-approval with straightforward W-2 income typically comes back within one to three business days once the lender has all your documents. Files with self-employment income, multiple properties, or complex financial situations take longer because the lender needs to build a complete picture of your income stability. Submitting all required documents upfront — rather than piecemeal — is the single biggest factor in a fast turnaround.

Does getting pre-approved for a mortgage hurt my credit score?

Yes, a mortgage pre-approval involves a hard credit inquiry, which typically causes a small, temporary drop — usually 5 points or less. However, mortgage-related hard inquiries within a short window (typically 14 to 45 days depending on the scoring model) are often treated as a single inquiry, so shopping multiple lenders in that window minimizes the impact. The short-term score dip is generally worth the benefit of knowing your real approval status before you make offers.
