VA Loan Closing Costs in Texas — What Veterans Actually Pay at the Table

Loan ProgramsBy Bond Peter NjokuJuly 14, 20268 min read

I'm Bond Peter Njoku, and one of the most common questions I get from veterans and active-duty buyers is some version of: "If VA loans require zero down, does that mean I show up to closing with nothing?" Not quite. Zero down payment doesn't mean zero closing costs — but VA loans have some of the most veteran-friendly closing cost rules of any loan program, and there are real ways to bring that number way down. Here's the honest breakdown.

What Closing Costs Actually Cover

Closing costs on any loan, VA included, cover the third-party costs of originating and closing your loan — not the down payment itself. On a VA loan in Texas, expect these categories:

  • VA funding fee — typically 1.25% to 3.3% of the loan amount, waived entirely for veterans receiving VA disability compensation
  • Appraisal fee — VA requires its own appraisal process, usually $600-$800 in Texas
  • Title insurance and escrow fees — required by Texas title companies to close any purchase
  • Lender origination and underwriting fees — varies by lender, I keep mine competitive
  • Prepaid items — first-year homeowners insurance premium, property tax escrow setup

How Veterans Minimize Out-of-Pocket Cash at Closing

This is where VA loans really shine compared to other programs:

  1. Ask for seller concessions. VA loans allow sellers to pay up to 4% of the loan amount toward closing costs and prepaids, on top of a separate allowance for standard costs like title fees. In today's buyer-friendly DFW market, many sellers are willing to negotiate this.
  2. Check your funding fee exemption. If you receive VA disability compensation, you're exempt from the funding fee entirely — this alone can save thousands.
  3. Roll the funding fee into your loan. Unlike most other closing costs, the VA funding fee can be financed into your loan amount instead of paid in cash.
  4. Ask about lender credits. A slightly higher rate in exchange for a lender credit toward closing costs can reduce your cash to close if you're tight on funds.

A Real Example

On a $320,000 VA purchase with a first-time-use funding fee at 2.15%, the funding fee alone is about $6,880 — but that gets financed into the loan, not paid in cash. Add roughly $6,000-$8,000 in other closing costs and prepaids, and a veteran negotiating 4% in seller concessions ($12,800) could realistically close with very little cash out of pocket, on top of already putting zero down.
