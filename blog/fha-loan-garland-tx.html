FHA Loan in Garland, TX — Low Down Payment for DFW Buyers

Loan Programs By Bond Peter Njoku July 10, 2026 6 min read

Garland, TX is one of the most active home-buying markets in the DFW metro — and for good reason. The city offers more affordable prices than Plano or Frisco while still providing access to major employment corridors, strong schools, and established neighborhoods. For buyers who don't have a large down payment saved, or who are working with a credit score below 700, an FHA loan is often the fastest path to homeownership in Garland.

This guide covers how FHA loans work in Garland, the 2026 loan limits for Dallas County, how to stack FHA with down payment assistance, and what to expect in the pre-approval process.

What Is an FHA Loan?

An FHA loan is a mortgage insured by the Federal Housing Administration, a division of HUD. Because the federal government insures the loan, FHA-approved lenders like Bond Peter Njoku (NMLS #2670329) can offer lower down payments and more flexible credit requirements than conventional loans. FHA does not lend money directly — it insures the lender against default, which allows lenders to take on borrowers who wouldn't qualify for conventional financing.

Key FHA features for Garland buyers:

  • Down payment: 3.5% with a 580+ credit score (10% if your score is 500–579)
  • Credit score minimum: 580 for 3.5% down (FHA-insured minimum — lender overlays may vary)
  • Debt-to-income ratio: Up to 57% with compensating factors in some cases
  • Loan limits: $498,257 for a single-family home in Dallas County in 2026
  • Occupancy: Primary residence only
  • Mortgage insurance: Required — 1.75% upfront MIP (rollable) + annual MIP of 0.55%–0.85%

FHA Loan Limits in Garland, TX for 2026

Garland sits in Dallas County. The FHA conforming loan limit for Dallas County in 2026 is $498,257 for a single-family home. This covers the large majority of Garland's home price range, which typically runs from $230,000 to $450,000 depending on neighborhood and size. Buyers looking at homes above the FHA limit would need to consider conventional financing or a jumbo loan.

FHA loan limits are also available for multi-unit properties: $637,950 (duplex), $771,125 (triplex), $958,350 (4-unit) in Dallas County — useful if you're purchasing a small multi-family property in Garland to house-hack.

FHA Loans vs. Conventional Loans in Garland

Both loan types work in Garland, but they serve different buyer profiles:

  • FHA wins when your credit score is 580–680, your down payment is small (3.5%), or you have higher debt relative to income. FHA underwriting is generally more flexible on these points.
  • Conventional wins when your credit score is 740+, you have 5–20% down, and you want to avoid mortgage insurance or remove it sooner. Conventional PMI is often cheaper than FHA MIP for strong-credit buyers.

The crossover point is typically around a 680–700 credit score and 5% down. Bond Peter Njoku (NMLS #2670329) runs both scenarios side-by-side to show you exactly which loan costs less monthly and over the life of the loan. Call 469-545-7180.

Stacking FHA with Down Payment Assistance in Garland

This is one of the most powerful combinations available to Garland buyers. Texas offers two major DPA programs that are compatible with FHA loans:

  • TSAHC Home Sweet Texas: 3–5% grant (never repaid) + FHA mortgage. No first-time buyer requirement. Income and purchase price limits apply.
  • TDHCA My First Texas Home: Up to 5% DPA (deferred, forgivable after 3 years) for first-time buyers and veterans. Income limits apply.
  • Dallas DHAP: Up to $60,000 in forgivable assistance for eligible Dallas homebuyers. For properties within city of Dallas limits.

A buyer using TSAHC's 5% DPA on an FHA loan effectively gets 100% financing — the DPA covers the 3.5% down payment plus part of closing costs. The result is a buyer who closes on a Garland home with minimal out-of-pocket cash. See the full guide to down payment assistance in DFW.

FHA Mortgage Insurance in Garland — What You Pay

FHA mortgage insurance has two components:

  • Upfront MIP: 1.75% of the loan amount, paid at closing or rolled into the loan. On a $300,000 FHA loan, this is $5,250.
  • Annual MIP: 0.55% for loans with 10%+ down; 0.85% for loans with less than 10% down, for loans above 90% LTV. Paid monthly. On a $300,000 loan with 3.5% down, annual MIP is about $212/month at the 0.85% rate.

FHA annual MIP currently lasts the life of the loan for most borrowers (loans with less than 10% down). If you want to remove MIP later, the typical strategy is to refinance into a conventional loan once you've built 20% equity in the home. The FHA Pre-Approval Process in Garland

Getting pre-approved for an FHA loan in Garland typically takes 24–48 hours once documents are submitted. Here's what to gather:

  • Government-issued ID (driver's license, passport)
  • Social Security number (for credit pull)
  • Last 2 years of W-2s or tax returns (self-employed buyers need 2 years of returns)
  • Last 30 days of pay stubs
  • Last 2 months of bank statements (all pages, all accounts)
  • Current landlord contact or 12-month rent history (if applicable)
