Moving to McKinney TX — Mortgage Guide for Relocating Buyers (2026)

RelocationBy PeterJuly 3, 20269 min read

McKinney, Texas has become one of the most talked-about relocation destinations in America. It keeps landing near the top of every "fastest-growing cities" list, and for good reason: top schools, a historic downtown, access to major corporate corridors, and home prices that — while they've risen significantly — still offer more space and value than nearby Frisco or Plano. If you're moving to McKinney and need to finance your home purchase, this guide covers everything: price ranges, neighborhoods, loan programs, and how to get pre-approved whether you're coming from across Texas or across the country.

Why People Are Moving to McKinney

  • McKinney ISD — consistently rated among the best school districts in Texas, with several nationally recognized high school programs
  • Historic Downtown Square — boutique shopping, restaurants, and a small-town feel uncommon in DFW suburbs
  • Lower price points than Frisco — comparable amenities, newer stock available, but median prices often $40,000–$80,000 below Frisco
  • Corporate proximity — 45 minutes to Dallas, easy access to Legacy/Frisco corporate campuses via US-75 and the President George Bush Turnpike
  • No state income tax — Texas's biggest financial draw for high earners relocating from California, New York, or Illinois
  • New construction — active new-build communities from Lennar, Highland, and D.R. Horton throughout north and east McKinney

McKinney Neighborhoods at a Glance

Stonebridge Ranch

McKinney's flagship master-planned community. Over 6,000 acres with lakes, trails, tennis, a beach club, and multiple pools. Homes range from $400,000 to well over $1 million. Multiple school zones within the development. Highly desirable for families with children.

Trinity Falls

Newer community on the west side of McKinney, built around the Trinity River greenbelt. Entry-level starts around $360,000 but new construction in the $480,000–$700,000 range is active. Amenities-heavy — resort pool, fitness center, dog park, community garden.

Eldorado / Craig Ranch Area

Established mid-range neighborhoods near Craig Ranch parkway. Excellent access to US-75. A mix of resale homes from the 2000s and 2010s with well-established schools. Typically $350,000–$550,000 for 3–4 bedrooms.

Downtown / Historic District

Older homes on larger lots; character architecture (craftsman, Victorian). More limited inventory. Prices vary widely — you can find a project home under $300,000 or a renovated historic home at $600,000+. Popular with remote workers who value walkability.

Loan Programs for McKinney Buyers

Conventional (Most Common in McKinney)

The 2026 conforming loan limit for Collin County is $806,500. With 5%–20% down and a 680+ credit score, conventional is the most flexible option for the McKinney price range. PMI is required until you reach 20% equity if you put less than 20% down, but it can be removed — unlike FHA's lifetime MIP on loans originated with less than 10% down.

VA Loans — Zero Down for Veterans

Veterans with full entitlement can purchase anywhere in McKinney with zero down payment and no loan limit. McKinney has a significant veteran and active-duty population given its proximity to Joint Reserve Base Fort Worth and Allen's defense corridors. I'm experienced with VA loans in Collin County and can walk you through the COE process and funding fee structure. VA Loan details →

FHA Loans — Lower Credit Score Requirement

The 2026 FHA loan limit for Collin County (McKinney's county) is $524,225. FHA requires just 3.5% down with a 580+ credit score, or 10% down with a 500–579 score. With McKinney's median price approaching $460,000, FHA covers the majority of the market. FHA Loan details →

Jumbo Loans — Above $806,500

For Stonebridge Ranch executive homes or custom builds above the conforming limit, jumbo financing is available. Typically requires 720+ credit score, 10%–20% down, and 12 months reserves. I can discuss current jumbo pricing and qualification criteria.

Getting Pre-Approved When You're Out of State

A growing percentage of McKinney buyers are relocating from out of state — from California (chasing tax relief), from the Midwest (following corporate relocations), and from the Northeast (cost of living). Buying remotely is completely normal in today's market, and I handle it routinely.

The remote pre-approval process:

  1. Initial call — 15–20 minutes to discuss your income, credit range, down payment, and timeline. No commitment required.
  2. Document upload — You send 2 years W-2s, 30 days paystubs, 2 months bank statements, and ID via secure link.
  3. Credit pull — With your authorization, I pull a tri-merge credit report and confirm your qualifying score.
  4. Pre-approval letter issued — Usually within 24–48 business hours, usable with any Texas Realtor.
  5. Remote offers — Your Realtor (I can refer trusted McKinney agents if needed) handles tours and offer submission. You can often sign everything via DocuSign.

Out-of-state income is fully acceptable — W-2, self-employed, or remote-work. If your employer is out of state but you're permanently relocating, that's handled the same as any W-2 income. Self-employed buyers will typically need 2 years tax returns and a year-to-date P&L.

Property Taxes in McKinney

McKinney (Collin County) property tax rates run approximately 2.0%–2.3% of assessed value annually, depending on school district attendance zone and special districts. On a $450,000 home that's roughly $9,000–$10,350/year, or $750–$863/month added to your principal and interest through escrow.

File for the Texas Homestead Exemption at collincad.org immediately after closing. It removes $100,000 from your home's taxable value for school taxes and caps annual assessed value increases at 10%.

What to Expect From the McKinney Market in 2026

McKinney's market has moderated from the 2021–2022 frenzy but remains competitive in popular neighborhoods. Key market dynamics in 2026:

  • Inventory has improved from pandemic-era lows — buyers have more choices and more time to decide
  • New construction in Trinity Falls, Painted Tree, and other active communities gives buyers a significant option outside resale
  • Multiple offers still occur on well-priced resale homes in Stonebridge Ranch under $550,000
  • Price cuts are more common above $700,000 where inventory has grown
  • Rate buy-downs and seller concessions are more negotiable than they were 2–3 years ago

Having a solid pre-approval and a responsive lender makes a real difference in competitive situations. Listing agents and sellers pay attention to who backs a buyer's offer.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are home prices in McKinney TX in 2026?

McKinney home prices range from around $280,000 for a starter townhome or smaller resale to $700,000–$1,000,000+ for executive homes in Stonebridge Ranch and custom communities. The median sale price in 2026 is approximately $440,000–$480,000. Conventional financing covers most of the market; jumbo loans are available for homes above $806,500.

Is McKinney TX a good place to buy a home?

McKinney consistently ranks among the best cities in Texas for families, quality of life, and long-term value. Strong schools, a growing job market across Collin County, an active new construction pipeline, and good long-term appreciation history make it a strong choice for buyers relocating to DFW. It's typically more affordable than Frisco while offering comparable amenities.

Can I use a VA loan to buy in McKinney TX?

Yes. McKinney is in Collin County within the Dallas-Fort Worth MSA. The 2026 conventional and VA conforming limit is $806,500. Veterans with full entitlement can purchase with zero down and no loan limit. I handle VA loans in Collin County. Call 469-545-7180 for a VA loan consultation.

How do I buy a home in McKinney TX remotely?

I handle fully remote pre-approvals via phone, email, and secure document upload — no in-person meeting required. Once pre-approved, you'll work with a McKinney Realtor who can tour homes via video call, submit offers digitally, and coordinate your closing remotely. Many out-of-state buyers close on McKinney homes without visiting until they pick up the keys.
