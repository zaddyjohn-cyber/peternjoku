FHA Loans — The Foundation for Most Dallas First-Time Buyers

For most Dallas first-time buyers I work with, the FHA loan is the foundation of the financing stack — and for good reason. FHA offers the lowest minimum credit score (580), the most flexible underwriting, and the ability to layer on top of nearly every down payment assistance program in Dallas. Here's what FHA delivers for Dallas buyers in 2026:

  • Minimum credit score: 580 for 3.5% down; 500–579 with 10% down
  • Down payment: 3.5% of purchase price
  • Loan limit (Dallas County): $524,225 for a single-family home
  • Mortgage insurance: 1.75% upfront (rolled in) + 0.55% annual MIP
  • DTI: Up to 50% with compensating factors
  • DPA compatibility: Works with DHAP, TSAHC, TDHCA, and most Dallas programs

On a $265,000 Dallas home with FHA financing, the down payment is $9,275. When I layer the Dallas DHAP on top, that $9,275 — and often the closing costs too — can be covered by the forgivable grant. My full FHA loans page covers the program in detail, and my post on FHA loan Dallas TX 2026 breaks down the specific Dallas County limits and requirements.

The Dallas DHAP — The Most Powerful FTHB Tool in DFW

I've already covered the Dallas DHAP in detail in my Dallas DPA guide, but it bears repeating here: up to $60,000 in forgivable assistance for first-time buyers purchasing within Dallas city limits, forgiven over 5 years, requiring no monthly payments. This is the best first-time homebuyer program in DFW, full stop.

The catch is the income limit (~$66,400 for a household of 4 at 80% AMI) and the geographic restriction (Dallas city limits only). If your income is above that limit or your target home is outside Dallas city limits, I pivot you to TSAHC or TDHCA — which serve a much wider buyer pool. Call or text me at 469-545-7180 and I'll tell you which programs match your situation within minutes.

What "First-Time Homebuyer" Actually Means in Dallas TX

One of the most common misconceptions I clear up for Dallas buyers is the definition of "first-time homebuyer." Most people assume it means you've never owned any home — that's not correct. For virtually every DPA program in Dallas:

First-time homebuyer = you have not owned a primary residence in the past 3 years.

So if you owned a condo in Austin six years ago and have been renting in Dallas since, you qualify as a first-time buyer for the DHAP, TDHCA, and most other programs. Divorced buyers who transferred their share of a home to a former spouse can also often qualify. TSAHC doesn't require first-time buyer status at all — any income-qualified buyer can use it.

I've helped plenty of buyers who assumed they "didn't qualify" and were pleasantly surprised. Don't disqualify yourself before calling — let me check. Text me at 469-545-7180.

Credit Score Requirements for Dallas First-Time Buyer Programs in 2026

Credit score is the most common barrier Dallas first-time buyers worry about. Here's the honest picture for 2026:

  • 580+: FHA loan eligible. DHAP + FHA combo available. TSAHC + FHA available. Most accessible path.
  • 620+: All programs available. Conventional 3% down eligible. Better rate pricing than 580–619 range.
  • 640+: Best FHA and conventional pricing. USDA eligible in rural-adjacent Dallas areas.
  • Below 580: FHA with 10% down (rarely practical for FTHBs); most DPA programs unavailable. I'll give you a 60–90 day credit improvement plan to reach 580.

If your score is below 580, that's not a dead end — it's a starting point. I've taken buyers from 540 to 580+ in under three months with targeted credit work. The key steps: dispute inaccuracies, pay down revolving balances, avoid new hard inquiries, and get added as an authorized user on a well-aged account with low utilization. I walk every client through this in detail. Call or text me at 469-545-7180.
