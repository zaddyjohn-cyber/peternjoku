USDA Loan Terrell TX 2026 — $0 Down in Kaufman County

USDA LoansBy Bond Peter NjokuJuly 15, 20267 min read

I'm Bond Peter Njoku (NMLS #2670329), and Terrell is one of the first cities I bring up when a buyer tells me they want to own a home but don't have a down payment. Terrell sits in Kaufman County, east of Dallas on US-80 — far enough from the metro core to qualify for USDA Rural Development financing, close enough that DFW jobs and amenities are still within reach. In 2026, most Terrell addresses are USDA-eligible, which means qualified buyers can purchase with zero down payment, no PMI, and a fixed rate. Here's everything you need to know.

Is Terrell, TX USDA-Eligible?

Yes — the majority of Terrell is USDA-eligible in 2026. The USDA Section 502 Guaranteed Loan Program designates eligible areas based on population and urban classification, and Terrell qualifies as a rural area under those criteria. That said, eligibility is address-specific. Boundaries can split a neighborhood, and an address two streets over from an eligible property may not qualify. I always verify the exact property address before we start a USDA application — don't skip that step.

To check any Terrell address yourself: go to eligibility.sc.egov.usda.gov, click "Single Family Housing Guaranteed," enter the address, and the map will show eligible or ineligible instantly.

USDA Income Limits for Terrell (Kaufman County) 2026

USDA income eligibility is based on household income — meaning every adult in the home counts, not just the borrowers on the loan. For Kaufman County in 2026, the limits are:

Household SizeIncome Limit
1–4 people~$112,450
5–8 people~$148,450

If your gross income looks slightly over the limit, don't assume you're out. USDA allows deductions — $480 per dependent child under 18, documented childcare costs for children under 12, and medical expense deductions for elderly or disabled household members. These deductions often bring buyers under the threshold. Call me and I'll run the calculation before you give up.

How a USDA Loan Works in Terrell

USDA loans are government-backed mortgages through the U.S. Department of Agriculture's Rural Development program. The lender makes the loan; USDA guarantees it if you default. That guarantee is what allows lenders like me to approve borrowers with no down payment and competitive rates.

There are two fees to know about:

  • Upfront guarantee fee: 1% of the loan amount. On a $230,000 loan that's $2,300 — but it can be rolled into the loan, so you don't need cash for it at closing.
  • Annual guarantee fee: 0.35% of the outstanding loan balance per year (~$67/month on a $230,000 loan). This replaces PMI and is significantly cheaper than FHA's mortgage insurance premium.

What the Property Must Meet

The home must be a primary residence — no investment properties or second homes. It must be a single-family dwelling in move-in condition. USDA has minimum property standards similar to FHA: the home must be safe, structurally sound, and sanitary. Most standard subdivision homes in Terrell pass without issue. Major fixer-uppers are harder to finance with USDA.

There is no maximum loan amount set by USDA — the limit comes from what you can afford within the program's debt-to-income guidelines, not an arbitrary ceiling.
