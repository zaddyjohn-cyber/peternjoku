VA Loan Lender in Garland, TX: What Veterans Need to Know

Loan Programs By Peter June 28, 2026 5 min read

The VA home loan benefit is one of the most powerful financial tools available to veterans and active-duty service members — and it's consistently underused. Many eligible buyers in Garland and across DFW don't realize they qualify, or assume the process is more complicated than it is. This guide covers who qualifies, what zero-down actually means for your out-of-pocket costs, and what to bring to your first conversation with a VA-approved lender.

Who Qualifies for a VA Loan in Texas?

The VA benefit is available to:

  • Active-duty service members who have served at least 90 consecutive days
  • Veterans with an honorable discharge who meet minimum service length (generally 90 days during wartime, 181 days during peacetime)
  • National Guard and Reserve members with at least 6 years of service, or 90 days of active duty under Title 10 orders
  • Surviving spouses of veterans who died in service or from a service-connected disability, if they have not remarried

Texas has one of the largest veteran populations in the country, and the DFW metro — including Garland, Mesquite, Plano, and Rockwall — has significant numbers of eligible buyers who have never used their VA benefit.

What "Zero Down" Actually Means

A VA loan requires no down payment for eligible buyers with full entitlement — meaning you can finance 100% of the purchase price. This is the program's signature feature and one of very few true zero-down options that does not require mortgage insurance.

However, zero down does not mean zero at closing. You will still have closing costs, which typically include:

  • VA funding fee: A one-time fee charged by the VA (currently 2.15% for first use with zero down for most veterans). This fee can be rolled into the loan balance, so you don't have to pay it upfront. Veterans with a service-connected disability rating of 10% or more are exempt from this fee entirely.
  • Title, appraisal, and lender fees: Standard closing costs similar to other loan types
  • Prepaid items: First year homeowners insurance, property taxes, and prepaid interest

The good news: VA guidelines allow sellers to pay up to 4% of the loan amount in concessions. In a negotiated purchase, many VA buyers structure the offer to have the seller cover closing costs, resulting in very little money out of pocket at closing.

VA Loan Limits in DFW for 2026

Since January 2020, veterans with full VA entitlement have no loan limit — you can borrow above the conforming loan limit ($806,500 in 2026) with zero down payment, as long as you can qualify for the payment. This makes VA loans exceptionally powerful in high-price DFW markets like Plano, Frisco, and Southlake.

Veterans with partial remaining entitlement (because they have an active VA loan on another property) may have a limit based on remaining entitlement. Your Certificate of Eligibility (COE) shows your current entitlement status.

VA Loan Timeline in Texas

A VA loan typically closes in 30–45 days — similar to FHA and conventional loans. The VA appraisal is the step that sometimes adds time, as VA appraisers must be VA-approved and assign based on the property's county. In busy markets, scheduling the VA appraisal early is important. Working with a lender experienced in VA loans helps — they know how to order the appraisal immediately and avoid unnecessary delays.

Documents Veterans Need to Start

Gathering these before your first lender call will speed up the process significantly:

  • DD-214 (Certificate of Release or Discharge from Active Duty) — for veterans
  • Statement of Service — for active-duty borrowers
  • Last 2 years of W-2s and federal tax returns
  • Last 30 days of pay stubs (or Leave and Earnings Statement for active duty)
  • Last 2–3 months of bank statements
  • Government-issued photo ID

Your lender will pull your Certificate of Eligibility (COE) directly from the VA system during the application process — you don't need to obtain it separately in most cases.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who qualifies for a VA loan in Texas?

VA loans are available to active-duty service members, veterans with an honorable discharge, National Guard and Reserve members who meet minimum service requirements, and surviving spouses of veterans who died in service or from a service-connected disability. Most veterans who served 90 consecutive days during wartime or 181 days during peacetime qualify. Texas has one of the highest veteran populations in the country, and the DFW area has a large number of eligible buyers who have not yet used their VA benefit.

Does zero down on a VA loan mean no closing costs?

Zero down means no down payment is required — but closing costs still apply. VA closing costs typically include a VA funding fee (which can be rolled into the loan), title fees, appraisal, and prepaid items like homeowners insurance and property taxes. However, sellers are allowed to pay up to 4% of the loan in concessions, and buyers can negotiate seller-paid closing costs. With the right offer structure, many VA buyers close with very little out of pocket.

Is there a VA loan limit in Texas in 2026?

Since 2020, VA loans no longer have a standard loan limit for veterans with full entitlement. If you have never used your VA benefit or have paid off a previous VA loan in full, you can borrow above the conforming loan limit with zero down payment. Veterans with remaining entitlement (a prior VA loan still active) may face limits based on their remaining entitlement. A VA-approved lender can calculate your exact entitlement from your Certificate of Eligibility.
