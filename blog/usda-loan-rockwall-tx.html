USDA Loan in Rockwall, TX — Zero Down for Eligible Buyers

Loan Programs By Bond Peter Njoku July 10, 2026 6 min read

Most DFW homebuyers assume USDA loans are only for rural farmland, far from the city. That assumption costs some buyers a zero-down financing option they didn't know they had. Parts of Rockwall County — including areas in and around Royse City — qualify as USDA Rural Development eligible, which means eligible buyers can finance 100% of the purchase price with no down payment required.

This guide covers which Rockwall County areas are USDA-eligible, how income limits work, what the loan process looks like, and how to check whether your specific target address qualifies.

Is Rockwall, TX USDA-Eligible?

The short answer: parts of Rockwall County are USDA-eligible, and some are not. The USDA draws eligibility maps based on population density and income data. Core Rockwall city (along Lake Ray Hubbard and the downtown area) has grown enough that portions no longer qualify. However, outer areas — particularly around Royse City, portions of Heath, and less-developed pockets of the county — continue to appear on USDA eligibility maps as of 2026.

USDA maps are updated periodically, and a specific address that was eligible last year may no longer be — or vice versa. The only way to confirm is to run the exact address through the USDA eligibility tool, which I do myself at no cost for every client considering a USDA loan. Call or text 469-545-7180 to check your address.

Royse City, TX — A Strong USDA Market

Royse City sits in Rockwall County and has been one of the most consistently USDA-eligible communities in the DFW metro. It combines suburban amenities — growing retail, schools, proximity to Dallas via I-30 — with rural classification for USDA purposes. Buyers who want more space, newer construction, and a lower price per square foot than inner Rockwall or Garland often find Royse City a compelling target, especially when 100% financing is on the table.

Forney (Kaufman County) is another USDA-eligible area nearby. See the full list of USDA-eligible areas near Dallas.

USDA Loan Income Limits for Rockwall County in 2026

USDA income limits are based on total household income — not just the borrower's income. Everyone living in the home who earns income counts, whether or not they are on the loan. Limits for Rockwall County in 2026:

  • 1–4 person household: approximately $110,650
  • 5–8 person household: approximately $146,050

These limits are set by USDA and updated annually — they change modestly from year to year. If your household income is close to the limit, it is worth having a lender calculate whether any deductions (child care expenses, disability expenses, dependents) could bring you within eligibility. I walk through this myself at no cost.

USDA Loan Requirements for Texas Buyers

  • Property eligibility: Must be in a USDA-eligible area (verified by address lookup)
  • Primary residence: USDA is for owner-occupied homes only — no investment properties or vacation homes
  • Household income limits: Total household income must fall within USDA limits for your county and household size
  • Credit score: Minimum 640 for automated underwriting approval with most lenders; manual underwriting available below 640 in some cases
  • Debt-to-income ratio: Typically 41% back-end DTI maximum, though exceptions exist with compensating factors
  • U.S. citizenship or eligible noncitizen status

USDA Loan Costs: What "Zero Down" Actually Means

Zero down means no down payment — but there are still costs to understand:

  • Upfront guarantee fee: 1% of the loan amount, charged by USDA. This can be rolled into the loan — you do not have to pay it out of pocket at closing.
  • Annual fee: 0.35% of the remaining loan balance per year, paid monthly as part of your mortgage payment. This is significantly lower than FHA's annual MIP.
  • Closing costs: Title fees, appraisal, prepaid homeowners insurance, property taxes. These cannot be rolled into the loan on a purchase, but sellers can cover them as concessions. Many USDA buyers negotiate seller-paid closing costs and arrive at closing with minimal out of pocket.

Bottom line: a well-structured USDA purchase in Rockwall County or Royse City can close with as little as a few hundred dollars out of pocket, depending on seller concessions and the strength of the negotiation.

USDA vs. FHA in Rockwall, TX — Which Is Better?

For buyers who qualify for both, USDA is usually the stronger option in eligible areas:

  • Down payment: USDA = 0% · FHA = 3.5%
  • Annual mortgage insurance: USDA = 0.35% · FHA = 0.55%–0.85%
  • Upfront fee: USDA = 1% (rollable) · FHA = 1.75% (rollable)
  • Property restriction: USDA requires eligible area · FHA has no area restriction
  • Income limit: USDA has household income cap · FHA has none

If the property is in a USDA-eligible area and your household income is within limits, USDA typically produces a lower monthly payment than FHA. See the full FHA vs. USDA comparison for Texas.
