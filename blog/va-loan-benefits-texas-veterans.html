VA Loan Benefits Every Texas Veteran Should Know

Loan Programs By Peter June 24, 2026 4 min read

The VA loan benefit is one of the strongest financing tools in the country, and it's earned. If you served — or you're a surviving spouse of someone who did — there's a good chance you qualify for a loan structure most civilian borrowers can't get. Here's what the VA loan benefit actually looks like for Texas Veterans.

What Makes the VA Loan Different

The Department of Veterans Affairs doesn't lend the money directly. It guarantees a portion of the loan to the lender, which lets lenders offer terms they couldn't otherwise:

  • No down payment for qualified borrowers within VA loan limits. Most other financing requires at least 3% down.
  • No monthly mortgage insurance. Unlike FHA or Conventional loans with PMI, VA loans skip that monthly cost entirely.
  • Competitive interest rates driven by the guarantee.
  • Flexible underwriting on residual income and debt-to-income — VA is one of the more forgiving programs on file structure.
  • Reusable benefit. Most Veterans can use VA financing more than once over their lifetime.

Who Qualifies in Texas

VA loan eligibility is based on service. The basics:

  • Veterans with qualifying active-duty service.
  • Active-duty service members who have served the minimum required time.
  • National Guard and Reserve members meeting specific service requirements.
  • Surviving spouses of Veterans who died in service or from a service-connected disability, in many cases.

Your Certificate of Eligibility (COE) confirms what you qualify for. We can pull the COE for you as part of your pre-approval — most Veterans don't need to chase it down themselves.

The Funding Fee

VA loans include a one-time funding fee that varies based on your service category, down payment, and whether it's your first use of the benefit. Some Veterans — especially those with a service-connected disability rating — are exempt. The funding fee can be financed into the loan, which keeps your cash-to-close low.

VA Loans for Texas Property

The VA loan works on most primary residences, including single-family homes, VA-approved condos, manufactured homes meeting VA criteria, and some new construction. The home must be your primary residence — VA loans aren't for vacation homes or pure investment property.

Texas Veterans also have a Texas-specific benefit through the Texas Veterans Land Board (VLB), which offers home loan, land loan, and home improvement loan programs alongside the federal VA benefit. We'll mention it when relevant to your situation.

How We Handle VA Files

VA underwriting is its own discipline. We've worked with enough Texas Veterans to know what underwriters want to see, how to structure residual income calculations, and how to handle situations where service dates or COE entitlements need clarification. The process should be respectful and clear — and the result should be a loan that puts your earned benefit to work for your family.

Thank you for your service. When you're ready, read more about our VA loan program or start a pre-approval. We'll confirm eligibility and walk you through next steps.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who qualifies for a VA loan in Texas?

VA loan eligibility in Texas is based on military service. Qualifying groups include: Veterans with sufficient active-duty service, active-duty service members who have served the minimum required period, National Guard and Reserve members meeting specific service requirements, and surviving spouses of Veterans who died in service or from a service-connected disability. Eligibility is confirmed through a Certificate of Eligibility (COE), which your lender can typically pull on your behalf during the pre-approval process.

Can I use my VA loan benefit more than once in Texas?

Yes. The VA loan benefit is reusable in most cases. Veterans who have paid off a previous VA loan can have their full entitlement restored. Veterans with an active VA loan can sometimes use remaining entitlement to purchase a second property simultaneously — a concept called bonus entitlement. The specifics depend on your remaining entitlement and the loan amounts involved. A lender experienced with VA files can review your COE and explain exactly what you qualify for.

What is the VA funding fee and who is exempt?

The VA funding fee is a one-time charge that helps fund the VA loan program. The amount varies based on your service category, down payment, and whether it's your first or subsequent use of the benefit — typically ranging from 1.25% to 3.3% of the loan amount. Veterans with a service-connected disability rating of 10% or more are exempt from the funding fee entirely. Active-duty Purple Heart recipients are also exempt. The fee can be financed into the loan, keeping your cash-to-close low.
