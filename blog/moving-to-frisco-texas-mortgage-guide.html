Moving to Frisco, TX in 2026: A Mortgage Guide for Relocating Buyers

TX Real EstateBy PeterJuly 3, 20268 min read

Frisco, TX has been one of the most searched "moving to" cities in America for several years running — and for good reason. Top-ranked schools, master-planned communities, world-class sports venues, and a booming job market have made it a magnet for families relocating from California, the Northeast, and the Pacific Northwest. If Frisco is your destination, this guide covers everything you need to know about the mortgage side of your move before you arrive.

Why So Many People Are Moving to Frisco

Frisco sits in Collin County, roughly 30 miles north of downtown Dallas along the Dallas North Tollway. What draws relocating families:

  • Frisco ISD — consistently ranked among the top school districts in Texas, with multiple campuses rated Exemplary by TEA
  • No state income tax — a major driver for relocations from California, New York, and Illinois
  • Corporate hub — headquarters of the Dallas Cowboys, FC Dallas, PGA of America, and major corporate campuses including Toyota, Liberty Mutual, and others
  • New construction — abundant new-build inventory in communities like Edgestone, Richwoods, and Phillips Creek Ranch
  • Suburban infrastructure — newer roads, retail, restaurants, and medical facilities built to match the population growth

What Homes Cost in Frisco in 2026

Frisco spans a wide price range depending on neighborhood, age of home, and lot size. Buyers should budget for:

  • Entry-level resale (older neighborhoods): $420,000–$550,000
  • Mid-range resale: $550,000–$750,000
  • New construction: $600,000–$900,000+
  • Luxury / custom: $900,000–$2M+

Frisco's price point puts most buyers into Conventional financing territory. The 2026 conforming loan limit is $806,500 — enough to cover a significant portion of Frisco's price range with standard down payments. Jumbo financing (above $806,500) is available for higher purchase prices with different qualifying standards.

Understanding Texas Property Taxes in Frisco

This is the number one shock for buyers relocating from low-tax states. Texas has no state income tax — but property taxes are among the highest in the nation, and they are a significant part of your monthly mortgage payment through escrow.

In Frisco (Collin County), property tax rates typically run 2.0%–2.5% of assessed value annually. Here's what that looks like on real purchase prices:

Home PriceEst. Annual Tax (2.2%)Added to Monthly Payment
$450,000~$9,900~$825/mo
$600,000~$13,200~$1,100/mo
$750,000~$16,500~$1,375/mo

Estimates only. Actual tax rates vary by year, taxing entity, and exemptions. Apply for your Homestead Exemption immediately after closing — it reduces your taxable value and caps annual increases at 10%.

Loan Programs for Frisco Buyers

Conventional loans are the most common in Frisco given the price range. Down payments from 3%–20%, credit guidelines typically 620+, and PMI that can be removed once you hit 80% LTV. The 2026 conforming limit of $806,500 covers a large portion of Frisco's market.

VA loans are an excellent option for Frisco's large veteran and active-duty community. Zero down payment with full entitlement, no PMI, and no loan amount cap — meaning VA financing works even on $700,000+ homes with no down payment if you qualify. Bond Peter Njoku pulls your Certificate of Eligibility at no charge.

Jumbo loans (above the conforming limit) are available for higher purchase prices. These have stricter credit, reserve, and income requirements than conforming loans. If your target price exceeds $806,500, ask about jumbo options.

FHA loans cap at $524,225 in Collin County — below most Frisco price points. FHA is better suited for entry-level resale or for buyers targeting more affordable DFW cities.

Out-of-State Income: What to Know

If you're relocating for a job that starts in Texas, lenders want to verify the job offer before closing. A signed offer letter and first pay stub (or evidence of employment start date) is typically acceptable. If you're remote-working for an out-of-state employer and keeping your current job, your existing income documentation applies — just confirm with your lender that remote work for an out-of-state company is acceptable under their guidelines.

Self-employed buyers who are relocating need the last 2 years of federal tax returns and a year-to-date profit & loss statement. If you're changing from W-2 to self-employment as part of the move, that transition adds complexity — address it with your loan officer early.

Homestead Exemption: File It the Day You Close

Texas law allows homeowners who occupy their primary residence to file for a Homestead Exemption, which reduces the property's taxable value by $100,000 for school district taxes (as of recent Texas legislation) and caps annual value increases at 10%. You must file with the Collin County Appraisal District after closing. This is free, takes about 10 minutes online, and saves most Frisco homeowners $1,500–$2,500+ per year in taxes once applied. Don't skip it.
