Texas Bootstrap Loan Program 2026 — 0% Down, $0 Closing Costs for DFW Buyers

Home BuyingBy Bond Peter NjokuJuly 15, 20269 min read

I'm Bond Peter Njoku, and one of the most underused down payment assistance programs I talk about with DFW buyers is the Texas Bootstrap Loan Program. Most people have never heard of it — it doesn't get the same marketing push as FHA or the My First Texas Home program, but for the right household it can mean getting into a home with 0% down and $0 closing costs. Here's how it actually works and who it's really for.

What the Texas Bootstrap Loan Program Is

The Texas Bootstrap Loan Program is administered by the Texas Department of Housing and Community Affairs (TDHCA) and is built specifically for very low-income Texas households who might not qualify for conventional financing on their own. It's structured around self-help and owner-builder construction models, often in partnership with local nonprofits, and it's designed to eliminate two of the biggest barriers to homeownership at once: the down payment and the closing costs.

Who Actually Qualifies

This program isn't for everyone, and I want to be upfront about that. Eligibility is generally capped at 60% of the area median income, which in DFW counties works out to a meaningfully lower threshold than programs like My First Texas Home. It also typically requires working with an approved nonprofit sponsor rather than shopping the open market the way you would with a standard purchase. If your household income is close to or under that threshold, it's worth a serious look. If you're above it, I'll steer you toward a program that actually fits.

What the Process Looks Like

Because Bootstrap works through nonprofit sponsors and often involves self-help construction, the process looks different from a typical resale purchase. You'll work with the sponsoring organization on the property and construction side while I handle the mortgage financing that ties it together. It takes longer than a standard FHA or conventional purchase — plan for a longer runway than the 30-45 days I'd quote for a typical resale closing in Garland, Forney, or Royse City.

If Bootstrap Isn't the Right Fit

Most of the DFW buyers I talk to about Bootstrap end up qualifying for a different down payment assistance program instead — TSAHC's grant doesn't require repayment and has no income cap as restrictive as Bootstrap's, and FHA loans paired with DPA get plenty of first-time buyers into homes across Dallas, Garland, and Rockwall counties with 3.5% down or less. I never want a buyer to think one program's requirements are the end of the road — there's almost always a path.

Documentation You'll Need to Get Started

Because Bootstrap is income-restricted, the application process leans heavier on documentation than a typical FHA or conventional file. Expect to provide recent pay stubs, two years of tax returns, bank statements, and household size verification so the sponsoring nonprofit and TDHCA can confirm you fall within the 60% area median income threshold. I always tell buyers to gather this paperwork early — incomplete income documentation is the single biggest reason any DPA application, Bootstrap included, gets delayed. If your income situation is a little complicated (multiple jobs, recent job change, self-employment), that's exactly the kind of thing I sort out with you before we ever submit anything.
