VA Loan Fort Worth TX 2026 — $0 Down for Tarrant County Veterans

VA LoansBy Bond Peter NjokuJuly 22, 20268 min read

I'm Bond Peter Njoku (NMLS #2670329), and Fort Worth is home to one of the largest veteran populations in Texas. Between Naval Air Station Joint Reserve Base Fort Worth (NAS JRB), the large number of veterans who settle in Tarrant County after separating, and military families relocating across DFW, Fort Worth has no shortage of buyers who earned VA benefits. The problem I see is that too many of them don't use it — either because they don't know how, or because a lender didn't bother to explain it clearly. This guide fixes that.

Why Fort Worth Veterans Should Use Their VA Benefit

Fort Worth home prices in 2026 typically range from $250,000 on the affordable end up to $500,000+ in western neighborhoods like Westover Hills or southwest Fort Worth. With a conventional loan at 3% down on a $320,000 home, you're looking at $9,600 upfront plus PMI (~$160/month). With VA — zero down, zero PMI, and competitive rates. That's roughly $9,600 you keep in your pocket at closing and $160/month you never pay. Over five years that's $18,000 in PMI savings alone.

Who Qualifies for a VA Loan in Fort Worth

VA eligibility is based on your military service record, not your income, credit score, or the property location. The broad categories are:

  • Veterans — Honorably discharged after meeting minimum service requirements (generally 90 days during wartime, 181 days during peacetime)
  • Active duty — Currently serving members with 90+ days of continuous active service, including NAS JRB Fort Worth personnel
  • National Guard and Reserve — Members with 6+ years of service OR those federally activated for 90+ days under Title 10 or 32 orders
  • Surviving spouses — Unremarried surviving spouses of veterans who died in service or from a service-connected disability

I obtain your Certificate of Eligibility (COE) through the VA portal at the start of the process — it typically takes minutes. If you've lost your DD-214, I'll walk you through how to request it from the National Personnel Records Center.

VA Loan Benefits Specific to Fort Worth

Fort Worth's VA loan environment has several advantages worth knowing:

  • No county loan limit — Veterans with full entitlement have no cap. Whether you're buying in Benbrook, Keller, or the Medical District, VA financing covers the full purchase price based on what you qualify for
  • Fort Worth HAP stacking — Some veterans also qualify for the Fort Worth Homebuyer Assistance Program (up to $25,000 forgivable), which can layer on top of VA to cover closing costs
  • VA IRRRL available — If you bought with VA previously, the streamline refinance (IRRRL) can reduce your rate with minimal paperwork and no new appraisal in most cases
