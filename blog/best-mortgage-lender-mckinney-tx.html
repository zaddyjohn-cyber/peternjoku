Best Mortgage Lender in McKinney, TX — What Actually Matters

Local Mortgage By Bond Peter Njoku July 10, 2026 6 min read

McKinney is one of the fastest-growing cities in the United States — and has been for over a decade. With master-planned communities, top-rated Collin County schools, and a mix of price points from starter townhomes to $800K+ custom builds, the McKinney housing market moves fast. Choosing the right mortgage lender here isn't just about getting the lowest rate on paper — it's about finding someone who can close reliably, communicate clearly, and knows what it takes to get an offer accepted in Collin County's competitive environment.

This guide breaks down what to look for in a McKinney mortgage lender, the loan programs available to local buyers, and how down payment assistance works in Collin County in 2026.

What Makes a Mortgage Lender "Best" for McKinney Buyers?

The word "best" in mortgage lending usually gets hijacked by whoever is advertising the most. Here is what actually differentiates lenders when you're buying in McKinney:

  • Speed and reliability: McKinney sellers — especially in master-planned communities like Craig Ranch, Stonebridge Ranch, and Painted Tree — will not wait for a slow lender. A pre-approval that takes a week is a problem when houses go under contract in 48 hours. Ask any lender you talk to: what is your average time from application to clear-to-close?
  • Program range: A lender who only offers conventional loans cannot help a first-time buyer who needs FHA, or a veteran who qualifies for VA. The best McKinney lender has access to all major programs and can tell you — before you start shopping — which one fits your situation.
  • Down payment assistance knowledge: TSAHC and TDHCA programs are actively used in McKinney. A lender who does not know these programs is leaving money on the table for their clients.
  • NMLS verification: Every mortgage loan originator in Texas must be individually licensed. Check any MLO at nmlsconsumeraccess.org before you share financial documents.

McKinney TX Housing Market: What Buyers Need to Know in 2026

McKinney's median home price has settled in the $430,000–$470,000 range after the peaks of 2021–2022. The market is more balanced than it was three years ago, but well-priced homes in good school zones still move quickly. Key facts for buyers:

  • FHA loan limit for Collin County in 2026: $498,257 — covers most entry and mid-level McKinney homes
  • VA loans: no loan limit for veterans with full entitlement — powerful in McKinney's price range
  • New construction: several active builder communities in McKinney offer incentive packages that can be stacked with mortgage programs — an experienced lender knows how to work with builder contracts
  • HOA fees in master-planned communities factor into your debt-to-income ratio — verify these before offer

FHA Loans in McKinney, TX

FHA loans are popular with McKinney first-time buyers and move-up buyers who want a lower down payment. Key requirements:

  • Minimum 580 credit score for 3.5% down
  • 500–579 credit score with 10% down
  • Debt-to-income ratio typically under 43% (some flexibility with strong compensating factors)
  • Property must be primary residence
  • FHA loan limit for Collin County: $498,257 (2026)

FHA can be combined with TSAHC down payment assistance to cover the 3.5% requirement, meaning some McKinney buyers close with little to no money out of pocket for the down payment.

VA Loans for McKinney Veterans

McKinney and Collin County have a significant active-duty and veteran population, particularly with proximity to bases in the greater DFW area. VA loans offer:

  • $0 down payment for eligible veterans and active-duty military
  • No monthly mortgage insurance (PMI)
  • No loan limit for veterans with full entitlement
  • Competitive interest rates — often lower than conventional
  • VA funding fee can be rolled into the loan (waived for veterans with service-connected disability of 10%+)

Many McKinney veterans are surprised to find their VA benefit applies to homes well above $500K without a down payment requirement. Bond Peter Njoku (NMLS #2670329) specializes in VA loans for Collin County buyers — call 469-545-7180 to verify your entitlement.

Down Payment Assistance for McKinney, TX Buyers in 2026

Two state-administered programs are actively available to McKinney homebuyers:

  • TSAHC Home Sweet Texas: A 3–5% grant that does not have to be repaid. Available to any qualified buyer — not just first-timers. Works with FHA, VA, USDA, and conventional loans. Income limits apply by county.
  • TDHCA My First Texas Home: Up to 5% DPA paired with a below-market 30-year mortgage rate. First-time buyers and veterans. Requires homebuyer education completion. Income and purchase price limits apply for Collin County.

These programs require working with an approved lender. Bond Peter Njoku (NMLS #2670329) is approved to originate loans under both programs. Learn more about down payment assistance in Texas or call 469-545-7180 to check your eligibility for free.

Conventional Loans in McKinney

McKinney buyers with 5–20% down and strong credit (680+) often find conventional loans competitive:

  • No upfront mortgage insurance premium (unlike FHA)
  • PMI drops off automatically when equity reaches 20%
  • Conforming loan limit in 2026: $806,500 — covers most McKinney homes
  • Jumbo loans available for purchases above conforming limit

How to Choose: 5 Questions to Ask Any McKinney Mortgage Lender

  1. Are you NMLS-licensed in Texas? (Verify at nmlsconsumeraccess.org)
  2. Do you offer FHA, VA, USDA, and conventional loans?
  3. Are you approved for TSAHC and TDHCA down payment assistance programs?
  4. What is your average time-to-close for purchases in Collin County?
  5. Will I work with you directly throughout the process?
