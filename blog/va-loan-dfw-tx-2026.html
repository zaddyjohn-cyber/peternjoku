VA Loan Eligibility for DFW Veterans in 2026

The VA loan is available to veterans, active duty service members, and surviving spouses who meet minimum service requirements. Here's a quick eligibility guide:

  • Active duty: 90 continuous days of service
  • Post-9/11 veterans: 90 days active duty or honorable discharge after 24 months
  • Gulf War era: 24 months active duty, or 90 days with early discharge for hardship or disability
  • National Guard / Reserves: 6 years of service, or 90 days under Title 10 or 32 orders
  • Surviving spouses: Available if veteran died in service or from service-connected disability and spouse hasn't remarried

I pull Certificates of Eligibility (COEs) directly through the VA portal — at no cost to you, as part of the pre-approval process. In most cases I can retrieve your COE in minutes. If your COE isn't in the VA system yet (common for recently discharged veterans), I'll walk you through requesting your DD-214 and getting it submitted. VA Loan Limits in DFW Counties — 2026

For veterans with full entitlement — meaning the VA benefit hasn't been used before, or a prior VA loan has been fully paid off and entitlement restored — there is no loan limit in 2026. You can finance a $600,000 DFW home with $0 down if your income supports it.

For veterans with remaining (bonus) entitlement, county conforming limits apply. All four major DFW counties are at the same 2026 conforming limit:

  • Dallas County: $806,500
  • Tarrant County: $806,500
  • Collin County: $806,500
  • Denton County: $806,500

This covers the vast majority of DFW purchases. For homes above $806,500, a down payment equal to 25% of the amount above the limit is required — still significantly less than a full jumbo down payment.

Getting pre-approved for a VA loan in DFW is straightforward when you work with a lender who knows the program. Here's what I need from you:
