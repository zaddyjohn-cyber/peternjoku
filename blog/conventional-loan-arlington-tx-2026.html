Conventional Loan Requirements in Arlington TX for 2026

Conventional loans follow Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac guidelines statewide. Here's what you need to qualify in Arlington in 2026:

  • Credit score: 620 minimum; 680+ recommended for the best rate tiers
  • Down payment: 3% minimum (HomeReady/Home Possible); 5–10% most common in Arlington
  • Debt-to-income ratio: Generally 43–45% max; up to 50% with compensating factors
  • Employment: 2 years of verifiable employment or self-employment history
  • Loan amount: Up to $806,500 (Tarrant County conforming limit, 2026)
  • Property type: Single-family, condos, townhomes, 2–4 unit properties

Arlington's housing stock includes a lot of well-maintained resale homes from the 1980s and 1990s, and conventional financing gives buyers more flexibility than FHA on the kind of minor condition issues that show up in an older home inspection.

Conventional vs. FHA in Arlington TX — Which Is Right for You?

I model both loan types for every Arlington buyer. My general rule of thumb for 2026:

  • Go conventional if: Credit score 680+, putting down 5% or more, buying a well-maintained resale
  • Consider FHA if: Credit score 580–639, minimal down payment, or property condition issues
  • VA loan if eligible: Always my first recommendation for eligible veterans — $0 down, no PMI

For Arlington's price range, the PMI-cancellation advantage of conventional financing usually wins out once a buyer's score clears 680 and they can put down at least 5%. I'll walk you through the 10-year cost comparison when we talk.
