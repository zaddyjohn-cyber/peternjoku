Down Payment Assistance in Arlington, TX — 2026 Programs for First-Time Buyers

First-Time BuyersBy Bond Peter NjokuJuly 14, 20267 min read

I'm Bond Peter Njoku, and Arlington is one of the more affordable entry points into DFW homeownership — but even affordable entry points require a down payment, and that's the single biggest hurdle for most first-time buyers I talk to. The good news: Texas has real down payment assistance programs available to Arlington buyers right now, and I'm an approved lender for the two biggest ones. Here's how they work.

TSAHC — Home Sweet Texas

The Texas State Affordable Housing Corporation runs Home Sweet Texas, open to all buyers meeting income and purchase price limits — not just first-timers. It provides up to 5% of your loan amount toward your down payment and closing costs, structured as either a grant (never repaid) or a deferred second lien forgiven after 3 years. Most Arlington buyers I work with take the grant option for simplicity. It pairs with FHA, VA, USDA, and conventional financing.

TDHCA — My First Texas Home

The Texas Department of Housing and Community Affairs pairs a below-market first mortgage with up to 5% down payment assistance at 0% interest, deferred for 30 years and forgiven if you stay in the home. It's available to first-time buyers (no ownership in the past 3 years) and veterans regardless of first-time status. A short homebuyer education course, usually 2-3 hours online, is required.

What Stacking DPA With Your Loan Looks Like in Practice

Take a $280,000 home in Arlington financed with FHA. A 3.5% down payment is $9,800. Using TSAHC's 5% grant covers that entire down payment with room left over for closing costs, meaning a qualified buyer could arrive at closing needing very little beyond prepaid interest and first-month escrow. This is exactly how these programs are designed to work — the state wants to get you into a home, and I know how to structure the offer correctly to make it happen.

Does DPA Affect Your Rate?

Usually, slightly — DPA-structured loans typically carry a rate around 0.25% to 0.5% higher than a standard loan without assistance. For most first-time buyers, having your down payment fully funded outweighs that small rate difference, especially when the alternative is continuing to rent while you save. Once you build equity, refinancing out of the DPA structure is always an option down the road.
