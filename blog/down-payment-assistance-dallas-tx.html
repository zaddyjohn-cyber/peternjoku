Down Payment Assistance Dallas TX 2026 — DHAP, TSAHC & How to Stack Them

DPA ProgramsBy Bond Peter NjokuJuly 18, 20268 min read

Dallas has one of the most generous down payment assistance ecosystems in Texas — and most buyers I talk to don't know the half of it. The Dallas DHAP — Up to $60,000 Forgivable

The Dallas Homebuyer Assistance Program is the crown jewel of DFW down payment assistance, and it's exclusively for buyers purchasing within the Dallas city limits. Here's what makes it exceptional:

  • Amount: Up to $60,000 in assistance — covers down payment and part of closing costs
  • Forgiveness: Forgivable over 5 years — stay in the home and the full amount is forgiven (no repayment)
  • Income limit: Household income at or below 80% of area median income (~$66,400 for a family of 4 in 2026)
  • Buyer requirement: First-time homebuyer (no ownership in 3 years)
  • Pairs with: FHA loans primarily; some conventional compatibility
  • Property location: Must be within Dallas city limits

I've used the Dallas DHAP with FHA loans to close buyers on homes in South Dallas, Oak Cliff, Pleasant Grove, Balch Springs-adjacent Dallas, and other affordable Dallas neighborhoods. On a $260,000 home with an FHA loan, the DHAP can cover the entire 3.5% down payment ($9,100) plus a significant chunk of the ~$7,000–$8,000 in closing costs — getting my client to closing with almost nothing out of pocket. Call or text me at 469-545-7180 to find out if your target home qualifies.

TSAHC Home Sweet Texas — The Statewide Grant

The Texas State Affordable Housing Corporation's Home Sweet Texas program is available statewide — no city-limits restriction — and offers a true grant of 3–5% of the loan amount that never needs to be repaid. Here's why I love this program for Dallas buyers who don't qualify for the DHAP or are buying outside Dallas city limits:

  • Amount: 3% or 5% of the loan amount as a cash grant
  • Repayment: Zero — it's a grant, not a loan
  • Income limit: ~$97,200 for Dallas County (much higher than DHAP)
  • First-time buyer: Not required — any buyer within income limits can use this
  • Pairs with: FHA, VA, USDA, and conventional loans

On a $280,000 FHA loan, a 5% TSAHC grant equals $14,000 — more than covering the FHA 3.5% down payment of $9,800. The difference can go toward closing costs. This is one of the most powerful and underutilized programs in Dallas County. See my full down payment assistance page for statewide program details.

TDHCA My First Texas Home — The Deferred Second

The Texas Department of Housing and Community Affairs offers the My First Texas Home program — up to 5% of the loan amount as a deferred second lien with a 0% interest rate, forgiven after 3 years. Key details:

  • Amount: Up to 5% of the loan amount
  • Terms: 0% interest, no monthly payments, forgiven after 3 years
  • Income limit: ~$97,200 for Dallas County
  • First-time buyer: Yes — or must not have owned a home in the past 3 years
  • Pairs with: FHA and VA primarily

My First Texas Home is particularly useful for buyers who want to keep more cash liquid at closing — it doesn't require them to bring the full down payment and costs, just a small portion. The 3-year forgiveness makes it a true no-cost benefit for buyers who plan to stay in their Dallas home. Also see my post on first-time homebuyer programs in Dallas TX for the full picture on FTHB resources.

How I Stack DPA Programs for Dallas Buyers

The real power comes from layering programs correctly. Here's a real example of how I structured a Dallas DHAP + FHA combination for a buyer in 2026:

  • Purchase price: $255,000 (Dallas city limits, Oak Cliff area)
  • FHA loan: $245,888 (after 3.5% down)
  • FHA down payment needed: $8,925
  • Estimated closing costs: $7,200
  • Total cash needed without DPA: ~$16,125
  • Dallas DHAP applied: $16,125 covered by forgivable grant
  • Buyer's out-of-pocket at closing: Near zero

That's a real homeowner with a real house in Dallas — not a hypothetical. The buyer has a monthly mortgage payment, builds equity, and in 5 years the DHAP is fully forgiven. The programs exist specifically for this scenario; my job is making sure buyers know about them and qualify correctly. Call or text me at 469-545-7180 and let's run your numbers.

DPA Eligibility Checklist for Dallas TX Buyers in 2026

Before I submit a DPA application, I walk every Dallas buyer through this checklist:

  • Credit score: 580+ for FHA + DPA combos; 620+ for conventional DPA
  • Income: Total household income under the program limit (varies by program — see table above)
  • First-time buyer: Required for DHAP and TDHCA; not required for TSAHC
  • Primary residence: All DPA programs require the home to be your primary residence
  • Homebuyer education: Most programs require a HUD-approved homebuyer education course — I'll point you to the right one
  • Property location: DHAP requires Dallas city limits; TSAHC and TDHCA work anywhere in Dallas County

Also check my Dallas mortgage page and FHA loan page for additional context on how these programs fit together. If you're buying outside Dallas city limits, read my post on down payment assistance across DFW in 2026 for a broader view.
