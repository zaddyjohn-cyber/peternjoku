VA Loan Eligibility for Irving TX Veterans in 2026

To use a VA loan in Irving, you need to meet VA service requirements and obtain a Certificate of Eligibility (COE). I pull COEs directly through the VA portal — it's part of my service, no extra cost to you. General eligibility thresholds include:

  • Active duty: 90 days continuous service
  • Post-9/11 veterans: 90 days active duty or discharge under honorable conditions after 24 months
  • Gulf War era: 24 months active duty, or 90 days if discharged early due to hardship/disability
  • National Guard / Reserves: 6 years of service, or 90 days active duty under Title 10 or 32
  • Surviving spouses: VA benefit available in certain cases

If you're not sure whether you qualify, text me at 469-545-7180. I've helped veterans untangle complex service histories and still get to the closing table with $0 down.

How Much Home Can a Veteran Buy in Irving TX With a VA Loan?

With full entitlement — meaning you haven't used your VA benefit before, or a prior VA loan has been paid off and the entitlement restored — there is no loan limit in 2026. For buyers with remaining (bonus) entitlement, Dallas County follows the 2026 conforming limit of $806,500. Loans above that threshold enter jumbo VA territory, which I can also navigate.

Irving's Las Colinas and Valley Ranch neighborhoods have homes priced $300,000–$550,000 — well within reach for most veterans using the VA benefit. Here's how I walk Irving veterans through the VA loan process in 2026:
