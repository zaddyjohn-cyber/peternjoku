Down Payment Assistance in DFW Texas 2026 — Complete Guide

DPA Programs By Bond Peter Njoku July 10, 2026 10 min read

The biggest barrier most DFW buyers face isn't the monthly payment — it's the cash they need at closing. A 3.5% FHA down payment on a $300,000 home is $10,500. Add closing costs and you're looking at $15,000–$20,000 out of pocket before you turn the key. Down payment assistance programs exist specifically to close that gap — and in 2026, DFW buyers have more options than ever.

This guide covers every major DPA program available to DFW homebuyers in 2026: state programs through TSAHC and TDHCA, city programs in Dallas and Fort Worth, USDA zero-down financing for eligible areas, and how to stack multiple programs to minimize your out-of-pocket cost.

Program 1: TSAHC Home Sweet Texas

The Texas State Affordable Housing Corporation (TSAHC) Home Sweet Texas program is the most widely used DPA in the DFW metro. It provides a 3–5% grant based on your loan amount — and because it's a grant, you never repay it. There is no second lien, no deferred payment, and no income restriction that limits you to a first-time buyer status.

Who qualifies?

  • Any buyer purchasing a primary residence in Texas
  • Income at or below the county income limit (varies by county — see below)
  • Credit score of 620 or higher (for FHA) or 640+ (for conventional)
  • Purchase price within TSAHC limits

TSAHC Income Limits for Major DFW Counties (2026)

  • Dallas County: ~$97,200
  • Tarrant County: ~$97,200
  • Collin County: ~$119,700
  • Denton County: ~$119,700
  • Rockwall County: ~$119,700

TSAHC limits are updated annually. Call Bond Peter Njoku (NMLS #2670329) at 469-545-7180 to verify current limits for your county before you begin shopping.

How TSAHC DPA is applied

On a $300,000 purchase with 5% DPA: the grant provides $15,000, which covers the 3.5% FHA down payment ($10,500) plus a portion of closing costs. Net out-of-pocket cost at closing can be as low as a few thousand dollars, depending on seller concessions and the negotiation.

TSAHC pairs with FHA, conventional, VA, and USDA loans. The DPA is disbursed by the lender at closing.

Program 2: TDHCA My First Texas Home

The Texas Department of Housing and Community Affairs (TDHCA) My First Texas Home program provides up to 5% of the loan amount as a deferred second lien. Unlike a grant, this is a loan — but it's at 0% interest and is fully forgivable after three years of occupancy (stay in the home 3 years and the DPA loan disappears).

Who qualifies?

  • First-time homebuyers — defined as anyone who has not owned a home in the past 3 years
  • Qualifying veterans — regardless of prior homeownership
  • Must meet TDHCA income limits for your county and household size
  • Must complete a HUD-approved homebuyer education course
  • Credit score minimum typically 620 or higher

TDHCA vs. TSAHC — which is better?

If you're a first-time buyer and expect to stay in the home at least 3 years, TDHCA often provides more assistance (up to 5%) and the deferred structure means it doesn't impact your monthly payment. TSAHC is the better choice if you are not a first-time buyer, or if you want a true grant with no strings attached. Program 3: Dallas Down Payment Assistance Program (DHAP)

The Dallas Homebuyer Assistance Program (DHAP) is the largest municipal DPA in North Texas, offering up to $60,000 in forgivable assistance to income-qualified buyers purchasing within Dallas city limits. This program is funded by the City of Dallas and administered through HUD Community Development Block Grant funds.

Key DHAP requirements

  • Property must be within Dallas city limits (not Garland, Mesquite, or other suburbs, even if they are in Dallas County)
  • First-time homebuyer requirement (no homeownership in past 3 years)
  • Income limits based on HUD area median income (AMI) — typically 80% AMI or below
  • Buyer must complete an 8-hour homebuyer education course
  • Forgivable over 5 years (20% per year)

DHAP's $60,000 maximum makes it one of the most powerful DPA programs in Texas — enough to cover the full down payment and all closing costs on many Dallas properties. However, availability is limited by annual appropriation and funds can run out. Apply early.

Program 4: Fort Worth Homebuyer Assistance Program (HAP)

Fort Worth offers its own municipal DPA: the Homebuyer Assistance Program (HAP), which provides up to $25,000 in forgivable assistance to income-qualified first-time buyers purchasing within Fort Worth city limits. The funds cover down payment and closing costs.

Key HAP requirements

  • Property must be within Fort Worth city limits
  • First-time homebuyer (no homeownership in past 3 years)
  • Income at or below 80% AMI for Tarrant County
  • Buyer must contribute a minimum of $1,000 toward purchase
  • Forgivable after a specified period (5 years)

Program 5: USDA Zero Down — For Eligible DFW Areas

For buyers purchasing in USDA Rural Development eligible areas, the down payment problem disappears entirely. USDA loans offer 100% financing — no down payment required. The tradeoff: the property must be in a designated rural or semi-rural area.

In the DFW metro, USDA-eligible areas include:

  • Royse City (Rockwall County)
  • Forney (Kaufman County)
  • Terrell (Kaufman County)
  • Parts of outer Rockwall County
  • Parts of outer Johnson County (south Fort Worth area)

USDA eligibility maps are updated by USDA and area boundaries can shift. Bond Peter Njoku (NMLS #2670329) verifies current address-level eligibility at no cost. See the full guide: USDA loans in Rockwall, TX.

Can You Stack Multiple DPA Programs?

In some cases, yes — but it depends on the specific programs. The most common stacking scenario for DFW buyers:

  • TSAHC grant + FHA loan: The grant covers the 3.5% FHA down payment and may cover part of closing costs. Seller concessions cover the rest. Net out-of-pocket: sometimes under $500.
  • TDHCA DPA + FHA loan: Works similarly — DPA covers the down payment; first-time buyers may also qualify for TDHCA's MCC (Mortgage Credit Certificate), which provides an annual federal tax credit equal to 20–40% of mortgage interest paid.
  • DHAP + FHA loan: Dallas buyers may use DHAP alongside an FHA or conventional loan, making this the most powerful combination in the city.

What Does "Forgivable" Actually Mean?

Several DPA programs use the word "forgivable" — here's what it means in practice. A forgivable loan is a second lien (a second mortgage on the property) that is forgiven — written off — after you occupy the home for a specified period without selling or refinancing. If you sell or refinance before the forgiveness period ends, you typically repay a prorated portion.

  • TDHCA My First Texas Home: forgiven after 3 years of occupancy
  • Dallas DHAP: forgiven at 20% per year over 5 years (stay 5 years = full forgiveness)
  • Fort Worth HAP: forgiven over 5 years

If you plan to stay in the home through the forgiveness period, a forgivable DPA is effectively free money. If you expect to move or refinance within 2–3 years, a grant (like TSAHC) with no repayment obligation is often the better choice.

Down Payment Assistance for Veterans in DFW

Veterans in DFW have the strongest set of options available:

  1. VA loan: 100% financing, no down payment, no monthly mortgage insurance. For most DFW veterans with VA entitlement, this is the best mortgage available — period. See the full VA loan guide.
  2. TDHCA My First Texas Home: Veterans are eligible regardless of prior homeownership — the "first-time" restriction is waived for those who have served.
  3. TSAHC Home Sweet Texas: Available to all income-qualified buyers including veterans, with no homeownership history restriction.

A veteran who doesn't use VA eligibility may still access TSAHC or TDHCA DPA on an FHA loan. Bond Peter Njoku (NMLS #2670329) compares VA vs. FHA+DPA for every veteran buyer to determine which option produces the lower total cost. Call 469-545-7180.

What Credit Score Do I Need for DPA in DFW?

DPA programs work alongside the underlying mortgage — so the credit score requirement depends on the loan type:

  • FHA + TSAHC or TDHCA: Minimum 620 credit score in most cases
  • Conventional + TSAHC: Minimum 640 credit score
  • Dallas DHAP or Fort Worth HAP: Typically 620+, varies by program year
  • USDA: Minimum 640 for automated underwriting; manual underwriting below 640 possible

If your credit score is below 620, you may not qualify for DPA programs yet — but that doesn't mean homeownership is out of reach.
