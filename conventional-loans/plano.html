Why Conventional in Plano?

The right loan for Collin County's move-up market

Plano is one of the most desirable cities in DFW — ranked among the safest large cities in America, home to several Fortune 500 and Fortune 1000 headquarters, and consistently producing some of the strongest home appreciation in Texas. The typical Plano buyer isn't a first-timer scraping together a down payment — they're a move-up buyer with solid income, established credit, and a target price between $400,000 and $650,000.

That profile is exactly where conventional loans shine. With a 680+ credit score, conventional pricing beats FHA on every metric that matters: lower monthly payment, lower total cost, and mortgage insurance that eventually disappears. There's no income limit (unlike USDA), no geographic restriction, and the 2026 Collin County conforming loan limit of $806,500 covers virtually every standard Plano purchase without going jumbo.

The 20% down myth is strong in a market like Plano — but it's not always the right call. Locking $96,000 in home equity that could stay liquid and working elsewhere is a real tradeoff. Many of my Plano buyers land at 10–15% down: enough to get a meaningfully lower PMI rate without wiping out their savings. I run the full break-even analysis so the decision is informed, not assumed.
