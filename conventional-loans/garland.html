Why Conventional in Garland?

When conventional beats FHA for Garland buyers

Garland is my home base — I know this market as well as anyone. Most Garland buyers are first-time or move-up buyers in the $220,000–$380,000 range. FHA is popular here because of low down payments, but for buyers with 680+ credit, conventional typically saves more money over a 5-year period — primarily because monthly PMI is lower and there's no upfront mortgage insurance premium of 1.75%.

The decision point is usually credit score. Below 660, FHA wins. Above 680, conventional wins. I'll run both for your exact file so you see the real numbers — not a general estimate.
