Why Conventional in Terrell?

Conventional makes sense for Terrell buyers who don't need $0 down

Most Terrell buyers I work with start by asking about USDA — and for good reason. USDA's $0 down and low annual fee are attractive at Terrell's price points. But buyers who earn above ~$112,450 (Kaufman County USDA income limit), or who've saved 5–20% and want to eliminate or minimize monthly insurance costs, benefit from conventional financing.

At 680+ credit on a $260,000 Terrell home, the monthly PMI on a conventional loan is roughly half of FHA MIP — and it cancels once you hit 80% LTV. The total insurance savings over 5 years often exceeds $8,000. I'll show you the exact comparison for your credit score and down payment.

  • PMI cancels at 78% LTV — USDA and FHA fees don't
  • No geographic address eligibility requirement
