Why Conventional in Prosper?

One of DFW's premier suburbs — the loan structure needs to match

Prosper sits north of Frisco along Preston Road and the Dallas North Tollway corridor, straddling Collin and Denton Counties. It's one of the wealthiest suburbs in Texas — the median household income consistently places it among the top DFW communities — and home prices reflect that: $500,000 to $900,000+ for most standard purchases, with luxury communities pushing well into seven figures.

For Prosper buyers shopping in the $500,000–$806,500 range, conventional conforming loans are typically the best structure — no upfront MI, PMI that cancels, and Fannie/Freddie underwriting that works smoothly at those price points. For purchases above $806,500, jumbo financing applies, and I handle that as well. The key is getting the right structure from the start — not discovering mid-contract that your loan type changes.

  • Minimum 620 credit (Prosper buyers typically 720+)
  • No upfront mortgage insurance

Many Prosper buyers don't realize they can stay within conforming limits by adjusting their down payment — which is worth doing because conforming loans carry better pricing than jumbo for most borrowers. On a $900,000 purchase, putting 10.8% down ($97,200) brings the loan to exactly $802,800 — within conforming range. That's a different monthly payment and a different rate than a $810,000 jumbo loan.

I work through these scenarios before you ever make an offer so there are no surprises at the loan estimate stage. Whether you're buying in Windsong Ranch, Star Trail, Light Farms, or an established Prosper neighborhood, I'll have your structure right before you go under contract.
