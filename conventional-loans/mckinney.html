Why Conventional in McKinney?

McKinney's growth attracts strong-credit buyers — conventional is built for them

McKinney has ranked among the best places to live in America for years running, drawing relocating families, corporate transferees, and Dallas professionals who want more space and better schools without leaving the DFW metro. The typical buyer has a strong income, established credit, and is targeting a home in the $350,000–$550,000 range. That buyer profile is exactly where conventional financing outperforms every alternative.

McKinney is in Collin County, which carries a 2026 conforming loan limit of $806,500 — high enough to cover virtually every standard McKinney purchase without going jumbo. And unlike FHA, conventional loans carry no income limit, no geographic restriction, and mortgage insurance that cancels once you build 20% equity. For buyers with 680+ credit, the total cost advantage over FHA is substantial.

  • Down payment from 3% to 20%+
  • No upfront mortgage insurance premium
  • PMI cancels at 78% LTV automatically
  • 2026 Collin County loan limit: $806,500

McKinney has more housing variety than most Collin County cities — from renovated craftsman bungalows near the historic downtown square to sprawling master-planned communities with resort-style amenities. That variety means the right loan structure depends heavily on where you're buying and how much equity you're bringing in.

Move-up buyers using equity from a prior home often have more flexibility on down payment than they realize. I help them model both scenarios — putting more down to eliminate PMI immediately versus preserving liquidity and letting the loan amortize toward the cancellation threshold — so the decision is based on real math, not assumptions.
