Why Conventional in Richardson?

Richardson's tech workforce is built for conventional financing

Richardson's Telecom Corridor — home to AT&T, Ericsson, Fujitsu, and dozens of tech firms — produces a buyer profile that fits conventional financing well: stable W-2 income, 700+ credit, and a $300,000–$500,000 target. Conventional delivers lower PMI at these credit scores, no upfront MIP, and a path to zero mortgage insurance once you hit 80% LTV.

Richardson straddles Dallas and Collin counties, but the conforming loan limit is identical in both ($806,500), so the county boundary doesn't affect conventional loan options. It matters for TSAHC income limits — but for conventional, you're comparing just rate and PMI, which I'll run side-by-side with FHA.
