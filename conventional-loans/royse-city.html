Why Conventional in Royse City?

When conventional beats USDA and FHA in Royse City

USDA is popular in Royse City — $0 down is hard to pass up. But USDA has income limits (~$112,450 for Royse City area households) and geographic restrictions. Buyers who've outgrown those limits, or who want to put 10–20% down to reduce their payment and build equity faster, find conventional the better long-term choice.

For buyers with 680+ credit on Royse City's $260K–$380K homes, conventional PMI is significantly lower than FHA MIP, no upfront MIP is rolled into the loan, and PMI cancels once you hit 78% LTV — benefits FHA can't match. I'll show you all three scenarios with your actual numbers.
