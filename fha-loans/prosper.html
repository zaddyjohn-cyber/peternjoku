Financing Options

The right loan for your Prosper target price

FHA — Entry-Level Prosper

For Prosper homes priced $450,000–$524,225 — smaller new construction, townhomes — FHA at 3.5% down is a valid option. TSAHC available for buyers under ~$119,700 income. Best for buyers with 580–679 credit targeting the accessible end of the Prosper new construction market.

Conventional — The Standard Path

For Prosper homes above $524,225 — most of the market — conventional financing is the primary path. Higher loan limits, lower PMI with strong credit (720+), and no FHA structure. Most Prosper buyers I work with use conventional with 5–20% down. I'll find the best rate for your credit profile and purchase price.

VA — No Loan Limit for Veterans

Eligible veterans can buy any Prosper home — at any price — with $0 down and no monthly mortgage insurance. A $575,000 Prosper home with $0 down vs. 10% conventional down = $57,500 in savings at closing. If you served, this is worth knowing before you settle on conventional. Call 469-545-7180.
