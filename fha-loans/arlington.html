Why FHA in Arlington?

Arlington sits between two major job markets — and FHA fits the buyer profile

Arlington is mid-DFW — halfway between Dallas and Fort Worth, with its own major employers (GM Financial, University of Texas Arlington, Texas Health Resources, Six Flags, Globe Life Park), and a housing market that still has room for first-time buyers. Homes in the $210,000–$360,000 range are within FHA limits for Tarrant County, and the combination of a large renter population and strong job base means buyer demand is steady.

Arlington doesn't have its own city-level DPA program the way Dallas and Fort Worth do, but TSAHC's statewide grant covers Arlington buyers cleanly — 3–5% of your loan amount, no repayment, no first-time buyer requirement. On a $260,000 FHA purchase, a 4% TSAHC grant covers $10,400 — more than your entire 3.5% down payment.

  • FHA: 3.5% down, 580+ credit score
  • TSAHC Home Sweet Texas: 3–5% grant, no repayment
  • TDHCA: up to 5% deferred, forgiven at year 3
  • Gift funds accepted for down payment
  • Strong employer base = solid long-term investment

Down Payment Help

DPA programs for Arlington FHA buyers

TSAHC Home Sweet Texas

A 3–5% grant on your loan amount — no repayment, ever. Statewide program covering all of Arlington. No first-time buyer requirement. Income limits apply (~$97,200 for Tarrant County in 2026). This is typically the strongest and simplest DPA option for Arlington buyers.

TDHCA My First Texas Home

Up to 5% of your loan as a deferred second-lien, forgiven at year 3. Pairs with a competitive FHA rate through TDHCA. For first-time buyers and Texas veterans in Arlington. Income limits apply. A solid choice if TSAHC grant isn't available or you want to maximize assistance.

Fort Worth HAP (select addresses)

A small portion of western Arlington may be eligible for the Fort Worth Homebuyer Assistance Program depending on city-limit boundaries. I'll verify your specific address — it's worth checking before we rule it out, since HAP provides up to $25,000 forgivable.

Arlington FHA Market

Where FHA buyers find homes in Arlington in 2026

Arlington's housing inventory for FHA buyers spans a wide range of neighborhoods and price points. The mid-city and south Arlington areas offer starter homes, while north Arlington near the Texas Rangers stadium corridor has seen steady appreciation. UTA students and staff often look east of campus. The west side near Fort Worth borders gives access to both cities' employment.

  • South Arlington — $200K–$290K, strong FHA inventory
  • Near UTA campus — steady demand, good rental upside
  • Mid-city Arlington — established neighborhoods, $230K–$320K
  • North Arlington — newer stock, $280K–$380K

Arlington also has a large active-duty and veteran population connected to NAS JRB Fort Worth and other nearby installations — if you've served, you may want to compare FHA and VA side by side. VA has no monthly mortgage insurance and $0 down. I'll show you both options so you can make an informed choice.
