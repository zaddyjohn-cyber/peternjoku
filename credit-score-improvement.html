Credit Help

Improve Your Credit Before Buying a Home

You don’t need perfect credit to buy a home — but stronger credit can mean stronger loan terms. Here’s a clear, no-judgment look at how to get yours ready.

Get Pre-ApprovedAsk us a Question
Why credit matters

How your score shapes your mortgage

Your credit profile affects which loan programs you qualify for, the interest rate offered, and whether mortgage insurance may apply. Even a small improvement can shift your options.

Better rates

Stronger credit can translate to more favorable terms, depending on your full profile.

More program options

Higher scores can open doors to Conventional and other programs alongside FHA.

Lower monthly cost

Better terms can mean a more comfortable monthly payment for your budget.

What helps

Ways to improve your score

Make on-time payments

Payment history is one of the biggest factors. Set up reminders or autopay for at least minimums to protect your record.

Pay down balances

Lower revolving balances reduce your credit utilization — often the fastest lever to improve your score.

Check your credit report

Errors happen. Pull your reports and dispute incorrect items so they don’t weigh down your profile.

Keep older accounts open

Length of credit history matters. Closing your oldest card can sometimes hurt rather than help.

What to avoid

Common mistakes before applying

Opening new credit lines

New accounts can lower your average account age and trigger hard inquiries.

Large purchases on credit

Furniture, a new car, or appliances on credit can increase debt-to-income before closing.

Closing old accounts

It can shorten your credit history and raise your utilization ratio.
